### 1. ★ SLT 2026 REAL-TSE Challenge: Real-world Target Speaker Extraction from Conversational Recordings — `2607.15198` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Shuai Wang, Zihan Qian, Ke Zhang, Jiangyu Han, Zikai Liu, Xiaoyang Yu, Haoyu Li, Marc Delcroix, **Kai Yu** ★, Lei Xie, Ming Li, Haizhou Li — SJTU X-LANCE, NWPU ASLP, Duke Kunshan, NTT (SLT 2026 satellite challenge) · eess.AS/cs.SD · 2026-07-16 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.15198)*

*Problem:* Target speaker extraction is usually benchmarked on simulated read-speech mixtures, so systems that look strong there fail on real conversational audio with natural overlap, reverberation, noise, and channel mismatch.
*Method:* An IEEE SLT 2026 challenge on real Mandarin/English recordings — given a mixture plus enrollment, recover only the target speaker — across an Online low-latency track and an Offline full-context track, scored by Token Error Rate, speaker similarity, DNSMOS, and target-activity F1.
*Result:* The overview reports task and dataset design, baselines, submitted systems, and condition-wise findings, establishing a realistic TSE testbed and lessons for future real-world extraction work.
**Why it ranked:** ★ followed author (Kai Yu) on a squarely on-profile task (target-speaker extraction / separation); a credible real-world benchmark from a heavyweight organizing team that pushes TSE past simulated read-speech — a reference the user will want on their radar.

### 2. ★ WanSong v1.0 Technical Report — `2607.14749` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Binghui Chen, Pandeng Li, Yu Liu, **Jingren Zhou** — Alibaba (Wan Team / Tongyi) ★ · eess.AS/cs.CV · 2026-07-16 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.14749)*

*Problem:* Music/song generation foundation models struggle to combine efficient inference, high-fidelity long-form audio, and controllability, and typically rely on autoregressive or cascaded AR-then-diffusion pipelines.
*Method:* WanSong is a pure diffusion model that directly generates commercial-grade, multilingual songs up to 5 minutes, outputting dual stems (vocals and background music) in a single run, with step-distillation for faster inference and an efficient path for fine-tuning and downstream editing.
*Result:* A single-stage diffusion recipe delivers long-form, high-fidelity, controllable song synthesis with separated stems and accelerated sampling.
**Why it ranked:** ★ followed lab (Alibaba Wan/Tongyi) and the cycle's standout release — a commercial-grade single-stage diffusion song generator with long-form, multilingual, dual-stem output; music-gen is a secondary interest, but the ambition, novelty vs AR/cascaded pipelines, and major-lab pedigree earn it a top slot.

### 3. RW-Voice-EQ Bench: A Real World Benchmark for Evaluating Voice AI Systems — `2607.14846` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
*David Ayllon, Alice Baird, Jeffrey Brooks, Franc Camps-Febrer, Jakub Piotr Cłapa, Theo Lebryk, Jens Madsen, Olya Ossipova, Sharath Rao, Hoon Shin, Tigran Soghbatyan, Georg Streich, et al. — Hume AI · cs.SD/cs.AI · 2026-07-16 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.14846)*

*Problem:* Voice AI benchmarks mostly measure isolated skills (WER, intelligibility, text-dialogue quality) and rarely test whether systems actually use the acoustic and paralinguistic information that separates speech from text.
*Method:* A multidimensional real-world benchmark spanning TTS, speech-to-speech (STS), speech understanding (SU), and ASR, scoring naturalness, expressiveness, identity stability, vocal-affect use, and robustness to accent/emotion/noise/conversational conditions.
*Result:* Performance is highly dimension-specific — some STS agents stay transcript-driven, TTS quality axes are largely independent, and real-world ASR conditions expose failures hidden by clean benchmarks — arguing voice AI be scored as a capability profile, not one aggregate number.
**Why it ranked:** Directly on the user's TTS/ASR/speech-understanding evaluation interests; a thoughtful multidimensional real-world benchmark from Hume AI (with a public leaderboard) that reframes voice-AI evaluation around acoustic/paralinguistic capability rather than a single score.

### 4. Large Audio Language Models for Spoofing-Aware Speaker Verification — `2607.14753` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Sofya Savelyeva, Mariia Perunova, Evgeny Kushnir, Artem Dvirniak, Dmitrii Korzh, Oleg Y. Rogov — AIRI / Skoltech (Moscow) · cs.SD/cs.AI · 2026-07-16 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.14753)*

*Problem:* Cheap, scalable TTS and voice cloning threaten speaker verification, yet spoofing-aware ASV (SASV) is dominated by modular ASV-CM fusion and cascades; whether large audio-language models can serve as a unified, auditable SASV backbone is unexplored.
*Method:* A systematic study of LALMs for SASV against conventional pipelines under zero-shot prompting, supervised adaptation, reasoning-oriented training, and reinforcement-learning-based optimization.
*Result:* Pretrained LALMs are near chance zero-shot (not natively suited), but task-specific adaptation closes the gap via several distinct routes, positioning LALMs as a promising, rationale-producing SASV foundation while clarifying where cascaded systems still lead.
**Why it ranked:** On-profile (speaker verification, anti-spoofing, audio-LLM) and the first systematic study of LALMs as a unified SASV backbone; a careful multi-regime evaluation with an honest picture of where LALMs help and where cascades still win.

### 5. Audio Diarization: A New Paradigm for Exploring Audio Recordings with Unknown Event Classes — `2607.12703` · **Relevance 6/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Alexander Werning, Reinhold Haeb-Umbach — Paderborn University · eess.AS · 2026-07-14 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.12703)*

*Problem:* In open-world audio monitoring the relevant sound-event classes are unknown in advance, so closed-set sound event detection cannot flag novel events.
*Method:* Borrowing from speaker diarization, the authors define "audio diarization" — detecting onset/offset times of overlapping sound events for an open set of classes without user prompts — as a first stage preceding classification, adapting a speaker-diarization system and proposing an evaluation setup.
*Result:* The approach matches a closed-set SED system's performance while additionally detecting novel, previously unseen sounds.
**Why it ranked:** A genuinely new task formulation (open-set, prompt-free sound-event localization framed like speaker diarization) from a credible group (Paderborn/Haeb-Umbach), on-profile for diarization/SED and useful for open-world audio monitoring — featured for the idea despite a modest first-step scope.
