### 1. Hierarchical Acoustic-Semantic Modeling for Full-Duplex SLMs (Lychee-FD) — `2607.06540` · **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 8/10**
Zhenyu Liu, Yunxin Li, Xuanyu Zhang, Qixun Teng, Shenyuan Jiang, Haolan Chen, Minjun Zhao, Fanbo Meng, Yu Xu, Yancheng He, Baotian Hu, Haizhou Li, Min Zhang — Harbin Institute of Technology, Shenzhen (Haizhou Li's group) · cs.CL · 2026-07-07 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.06540)
*Problem:* Native full-duplex spoken language models suffer "modality interference" — jointly modeling acoustics and semantics in one shared parameter space degrades knowledge and semantic integrity, making the models feel unnatural and unintelligent.
*Method:* A fine-grained analysis of optimization dynamics traces the degradation to gradient conflicts between acoustic and semantic modeling; Lychee-FD then applies a hierarchical parameter-separation strategy that decouples the conflicting modalities in deep layers while a dedicated semantic-alignment channel preserves cross-modal coherence.
*Result:* SOTA on multiple full-duplex benchmarks — +7.4% Spoken QA and +28.5% on FullDuplexBench 1.5 — without hurting inference efficiency.
**Why it ranked:** Dead-center of the user's spoken-language-model interest and the hottest speech-LM frontier; a rigorous root-cause diagnosis (gradient conflict) plus an elegant fix and concrete SOTA numbers from Haizhou Li's group is exactly the kind of paper a speech group circulates.

### 2. ★ Escaping the Procrustean Bed: Groupwise Orthogonal Connectors for Audio-Language Models (ORCA) — `2607.06014` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 8/10**
Ho-Lam Chung, Ke-Han Lu, Yi-Cheng Lin, Guan-Ting Lin, Yiming Chen, Hung-yi Lee — National Taiwan University (NTU) · cs.SD · 2026-07-07 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.06014)
*Problem:* In audio-language models, the Q-Former connector that compresses the speech encoder's output before the LLM collapses its output vectors to a single direction, making different speakers nearly indistinguishable and discarding paralinguistic cues (speaker identity, gender, prosody).
*Method:* ORCA splits the connector's queries into groups whose outputs are constrained to point in different (orthogonal) directions, reversing the representational collapse without enlarging the model.
*Result:* +26.4 points on SAKURA multi-hop reasoning, reaching 75.2% (vs. 49.0% for the 8B Audio Flamingo-3) with a 4B model, while cutting query redundancy 12× and raising cross-speaker variance 75×.
**Why it ranked:** ★ followed (Hung-yi Lee) and a sharp, quotable result on the audio-LLM frontier the user tracks — a crisp diagnosis of connector collapse plus an elegant orthogonality fix that lets a smaller model beat a larger one by a wide margin.

### 3. ★ Fréchet Distance Loss on Speech Representations for Text-to-Speech (SR-FD) — `2607.06027` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 8/10**
Ho-Lam Chung, Kuan-Po Huang, Bo-Ru Lu, Hung-yi Lee — National Taiwan University (NTU) · cs.SD · 2026-07-07 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.06027)
*Problem:* Few-step diffusion / flow-matching TTS models train only with local objectives (flow matching, reconstruction, stop prediction) that never ask whether sampled speech follows the distribution of high-quality speech.
*Method:* SR-FD is a training-time distributional regularizer for tokenizer-free flow-matching AR TTS: during fine-tuning the model synthesizes with its deployment-time few-step sampler, and SR-FD matches the mean/covariance of frozen Whisper and CTC features to offline reference statistics — no discriminator and no inference-time cost.
*Result:* On Seed-TTS English, 4-step fine-tuning cuts WER from 2.23% to 1.41% (36.5% relative), beating even the original 10-step baseline (1.74%), with speaker similarity preserved and gains significant under a paired bootstrap.
**Why it ranked:** ★ followed (Hung-yi Lee) and standout core-TTS work — a genuinely novel, discriminator-free distributional loss that is free at inference and posts a strong, statistically-tested intelligibility gain; the kind of clean, reusable method a TTS group adopts.

### 4. Speaker-Disentangled Chunk-Wise Regression for Syllabic Tokenization — `2607.04064` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
Ryota Komatsu, Kota Kawakita, Takuma Okamoto, Takahiro Shinozaki — Institute of Science Tokyo (Shinozaki Lab) & NICT · cs.CL, cs.AI, cs.SD, eess.AS · 2026-07-05 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.04064)
*Problem:* Unsupervised syllabic tokenizers built by distilling HuBERT with an utterance-level cross-entropy objective end up predicting speaker identity rather than linguistic content, polluting the resulting syllabic tokens.
*Method:* A speaker-disentangled tokenizer regresses speaker-perturbed student representations toward clean teacher targets within fixed-length chunks, removing the speaker leakage while retaining content structure.
*Result:* SOTA syllable-boundary detection and syllabic-segment clustering, and a speech LM trained on these tokens gains 7% relative on syntactic/semantic understanding over the phone-level SpiRit-LM (accepted to IEEE OJSP).
**Why it ranked:** Squarely on the speech-tokenizer / spoken-LM axis the user prioritizes — a clean, well-motivated disentanglement idea with SOTA tokenization and a concrete downstream SLM improvement over a known baseline, in a peer-reviewed venue.

### 5. WordVoice: Explicit and Decoupled Multi-Dimensional Word-Level Control for LLM-Based TTS — `2607.06461` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
Sihang Nie, Jinxin Ji, Xiaofen Xing, Deyi Tuo, Chengbin Jin, Jialong Mai, Xiangmin Xu — South China University of Technology (SCUT) · eess.AS, cs.CL, cs.SD · 2026-07-07 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.06461)
*Problem:* LLM-based TTS is natural but only coarsely controllable; use cases like audiobook narration and video dubbing need explicit, temporally-aligned word-level control that implicit end-to-end generation can't provide, hindered by scarce fine-grained annotations.
*Method:* The authors release WordVoice-5A, a 4.7k-hour bilingual corpus with five-dimensional word-level labels (duration, boundary, energy, pitch, tone), and introduce a bound-token "acoustic planning" mechanism inside the LLM plus a fine-grained acoustic-modulation module bridging discrete tokens to the waveform.
*Result:* Superior decoupled control over multiple word-level acoustic dimensions while keeping competitive zero-shot synthesis stability; code and audio samples released.
**Why it ranked:** Directly serves the TTS / prosody-control interest with a practical, broadly-applicable contribution — an explicit word-level control paradigm backed by a large, openly-released fine-grained dataset and public demo, the scarce ingredient controllable-TTS work is usually bottlenecked on.
