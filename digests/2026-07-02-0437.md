### ★ 1. Do Multimodal Large Language Models Need Reasoning to Classify Dementia from Speech? — 2607.00260 · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7.5/10**
**Authors:** Liming Wang, Neguine Rezaii, Bradford C. Dickerson, James Glass — MIT CSAIL & Massachusetts General Hospital / Harvard Medical School · eess.AS · 2026-06-30 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.00260)
*Problem:* It is unclear whether reasoning MLLMs actually help automatic dementia classification (ADC) from voice, or how their reasoning should be used.
*Method:* Careful evaluation of reasoning MLLMs for ADC, then DeTAiL — an adaptor + reinforcement-learning framework that exploits the model's internal representations instead of its text rationales.
*Result:* Text-based rationales hallucinate inconsistent diagnoses and underperform LLM-free baselines; DeTAiL consistently beats strong baselines across two dementia datasets with different formats and label granularities.
**Why it ranked:** ★ followed lab (MIT CSAIL, James Glass) squarely on the audio-LLM interest — an honest, rigorous study exposing that MLLM "reasoning" traces mislead clinical speech classification, plus a representation-level fix that works.

### 2. Beyond Cross-Reconstruction: Probing-Based Disentanglement Evaluation for Acoustic Teleportation Codecs — 2606.31365 · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 8/10**
**Authors:** Philipp Grundhuber, Emanuël A. P. Habets — Fraunhofer IIS & International Audio Laboratories Erlangen · eess.AS, cs.SD · 2026-06-30 · Interspeech 2026 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.31365)
*Problem:* Codecs that split speech into content/speaker/acoustics subspaces are judged by cross-reconstruction, which can't detect leakage across partitions.
*Method:* Extend a probing framework — regress room-acoustic parameters (RT60, clarity, direct-to-reverberant ratio) and classify speaker identity, scoring disentanglement as the gap between intended and unintended partitions.
*Result:* On an acoustic-teleportation codec, speaker identity stays confined to its partition but acoustics leak into speech embeddings; acoustic embeddings blindly estimate room parameters within 0.02 s of supervised baselines.
**Why it ranked:** Core codec/disentanglement interest with a principled, leakage-sensitive evaluation from Habets' well-regarded lab; the emergent room-acoustic structure finding makes it the strongest paper of the window.

### 3. Enhancing Flow Matching with A Unified Guidance Framework for Efficient and Robust Speech Synthesis — 2607.00363 · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7.5/10**
**Authors:** Zuda Yu, Qianhui Xu, Ting Chen, Junhui Zhang, Tao Fu, Hongjiang Yu, Qiangqing Wang, Yang Song — Zuoyebang · cs.SD, cs.AI · 2026-07-01 · Interspeech 2026 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.00363)
*Problem:* Flow-matching speech synthesis is held back by high inference latency and timbre leakage.
*Method:* A unified guidance framework: Data-guidance via heterogeneous augmentation to disentangle content from acoustic residue, plus Model-guidance fusing trajectory rectification with an intrinsic guidance objective that removes classifier-free-guidance overhead.
*Result:* Roughly 3× faster inference while improving speaker similarity over state-of-the-art baselines.
**Why it ranked:** Squarely on core TTS/flow-matching interest with a concrete efficiency-plus-robustness win (3× speedup, better speaker similarity) and Interspeech acceptance; a practically deployable industry contribution.

### 4. NPUsper: Eliminating Redundant Computation for Real-Time Whisper on Mobile NPUs — 2607.01108 · **Relevance 7.5/10 · Quality 7.5/10**
**Authors:** Sihyeon Lee, Hojeong Lee, Sungwon Woo, Chengpo Yan, Suman Banerjee, Seyeon Kim — Korea University & University of Wisconsin–Madison · cs.SD · 2026-07-01 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.01108)
*Problem:* Live Whisper transcription on mobile NPUs wastes compute on heavy padding and redundant autoregressive decoding.
*Method:* Detect hallucinated tokens online from decoder cross-attention patterns to process short chunks with minimal carryover, plus "controlled unrolling" that runs decoding as K-step chunk graphs to cut KV-cache and dispatch overhead.
*Result:* Up to 4.84× lower per-word latency, 33.2× lower time-to-first-token, and 88.64% lower power vs baselines at comparable accuracy; code released.
**Why it ranked:** On-interest streaming-ASR/Whisper efficiency with striking, reproducible on-device gains and released code — a strong systems-meets-speech contribution.

### 5. A Geometric Perspective on Composable Emotion Steering in Text-to-Speech Models — 2607.00946 · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
**Authors:** Siyi Wang, James Bailey, Ting Dang — University of Melbourne · cs.SD, cs.LG · 2026-07-01 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.00946)
*Problem:* The representation geometry of emotion-control modules in hybrid TTS — and what it implies for steerability — is poorly understood.
*Method:* First comparative study of speech-LM (SLM) vs conditional flow-matching (CFM) modules as activation-steering sites for mixed-emotion synthesis, characterizing subspaces via linear probing and local intrinsic dimensionality, then testing single-site and joint steering.
*Result:* SLM offers a clean, low-dimensional emotion subspace with strong speaker–emotion disentanglement; CFM entangles them and generalizes poorly across speakers; joint steering raises intensity but hurts proportional control and quality.
**Why it ranked:** Directly on core interest (expressive/controllable TTS) with a novel geometry-first framing of where to steer emotion in hybrid systems — an analysis rather than a new model, but genuinely useful design guidance.
