### 1. ★ SALMONN-2: Advancing General-Purpose Hearing Abilities with Self-Supervised Representations — `2607.17079` · **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 9/10**
*Xiaoyu Yang, Xuenan Xu, Wenyi Yu, Changli Tang, Wen Wu, Guangzhi Sun, **Chao Zhang** ★, **Philip Woodland** ★ — Tsinghua University, University of Cambridge & Shanghai AI Laboratory · eess.AS · 2026-07-19 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.17079)*

*Problem:* Audio LLMs are usually built on heavily supervised audio encoders; whether general-purpose self-supervised (SSL) representations can serve as a stronger, more balanced foundation is untested.
*Method:* An ALLM on a unified SSL encoder, with a multi-layer feature-fusion (MLF) adapter aggregating all encoder layers before the LM, plus contextual-biasing training to acquire multimodal in-context learning (MICL).
*Result:* The SSL encoder matches or beats specialised supervised encoders — more balanced across speech/audio/music/paralinguistics — and sets SOTA among comparable open-weight ALLMs on MMAU-Pro, MMAR and MMSU; MICL is shown to be trainable, not emergent.
**Why it ranked:** Flagship general-purpose audio-LLM line from followed authors Chao Zhang (Tsinghua) and Philip Woodland (Cambridge); a genuinely new SSL-encoder foundation with SOTA open-weight results and a crisp MICL insight — the cycle's headline read.

### 2. ★ Audio-Visual Flamingo: Open Audio-Visual Intelligence for Long and Complex Videos — `2607.16107` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 9/10**
***Sreyan Ghosh** ★, Arushi Goel, Zhifeng Kong, S Sakshi, Ramani Duraiswami, **Dinesh Manocha** ★, Mohammad Shoeybi, Bryan Catanzaro, Wei Ping — NVIDIA & University of Maryland · eess.AS/cs.CV · 2026-07-17 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.16107)*

*Problem:* Most audio-visual LLMs handle only short clips and stumble on long, complex real-world videos that demand temporal, compositional and cross-modal reasoning.
*Method:* A fully-open AV-LLM trained on ~7M audio-visual caption/QA instances via a three-stage short→long curriculum, with a Temporal Audio-Visual Interleaved chain-of-thought that grounds intermediate reasoning steps to timestamps.
*Result:* Beats similarly-sized open models across 15+ audio/AV/omni benchmarks and rivals or surpasses much larger open-weight and closed models on long, complex real-world understanding, with strong zero-shot transfer to unseen tasks.
**Why it ranked:** Fully-open SOTA AV-LLM from NVIDIA + University of Maryland with followed authors Sreyan Ghosh and Dinesh Manocha; ambitious scale, a novel timestamp-grounded reasoning framework, and everything released — a standout on audio-visual understanding.

### 3. ★ X-Translator: A Real-Time Multilingual Speaker-Aware Speech-to-Speech Translation System — `2607.17544` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Yuxiang Zhao, Yichi Zhang, Yushen Chen, Keqi Deng, Shuai Fan, **Kai Yu** ★, **Xie Chen** ★ — SJTU X-LANCE (Shanghai Jiao Tong University), Shanghai Innovation Institute, Microsoft & AISpeech · eess.AS · 2026-07-20 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.17544)*

*Problem:* Open, reproducible real-time speech-to-speech translation struggles in long-form multi-speaker conversations, where partial ASR hypotheses are unstable, turn boundaries blur, and target speech must match each speaker's voice.
*Method:* A low-cost modular cascade — streaming ASR, machine translation, prompt-conditioned TTS — coordinated by a session-level runtime controller, using incremental segment commitment and an online speaker-prompt manager to bind source spans to voice prompts.
*Result:* An open platform evaluated on OpenSTBench for translation, latency, long-form voice stability and multi-speaker speaker preservation, benchmarked against proprietary APIs; code and demo released.
**Why it ranked:** Followed authors Kai Yu and Xie Chen (SJTU X-LANCE) on a core speech-translation/TTS topic; an open, deployment-oriented S2ST platform that fills a real reproducibility gap — engineering-forward (a cascade rather than a new model), but useful and reproducible.

### 4. ★ Is One Score Enough? Assessing Singing Quality of Songs with Temporal Score Curves — `2607.16599` · **Relevance 6/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Yishan Lv, Jing Luo, Xinyu Yang, **Zhizheng Wu** ★ — Xi'an Jiaotong University & The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen · cs.SD/cs.MM/eess.AS · 2026-07-18 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.16599)*

*Problem:* Singing-quality assessment targets short clips; full-length songs need modeling how quality varies across segments and shapes the overall score, but segment-level annotations are scarce.
*Method:* SongSQA — a Segment Score Predictor trained on teacher-generated pseudo-labels for segment-level prediction, then a Song Quality Aggregator that fuses segment features/scores via a learnable song embedding and self-attention, yielding a holistic score plus a temporal quality curve.
*Result:* Up to 13.95% relative KTAU improvement over the strongest baseline, with consistent gains across other metrics and all datasets.
**Why it ranked:** Followed author Zhizheng Wu (CUHK-Shenzhen); singing/music evaluation is only a secondary interest, but the full-song formulation, pseudo-label supervision and interpretable temporal quality curve make it a tidy, genuinely useful contribution.

### 5. ★ Controlling Implicit Shortcut Reliance in L2 Spoken English Auto-markers — `2607.16085` · **Relevance 6/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Shilin Gao, **Mark J. F. Gales** ★, **Kate M. Knill** ★ — University of Cambridge (ALTA Institute) · cs.CL/eess.AS · 2026-07-17 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.16085)*

*Problem:* End-to-end speech/text proficiency graders learn "shortcuts," over-relying on exploitable input features — enabling malpractice where learners raise their score by gaming the shortcut rather than improving ability.
*Method:* A novel training criterion that reduces a classifier's reliance on shortcut features, demonstrated on two proficiency graders — one operating on audio, the other on ASR transcripts.
*Result:* Both systems show higher-than-reference correlation with exploitable features (evidence of over-reliance); the modified criterion pulls that correlation back toward the human-reference level, curbing the malpractice route.
**Why it ranked:** Two followed authors — Mark J. F. Gales and Kate M. Knill (Cambridge ALTA); a rigorous, assessment-integrity take on shortcut/spurious-feature learning in speech graders — narrow in scope, but from a top spoken-language-assessment group.

Core codec interest — a clean idea that just missed the featured list.
- **Do Speech Tokens Leak Voiceprints? Speaker Inversion Attacks Against End-to-End Speech LMs** (`2607.16870`): formulates a speaker-inversion attack (an "Audio BERT" embedder + two-stage SpInv) showing the discrete speech tokens fed to Moshi, Higgs3, Kimi-Audio and Qwen3-Omni leak speaker identity — cosine >0.70 in an attacker-specified encoder space from just 3s of frontend output; a timely privacy warning for speech-LM builders. (Affiliations not stated on the preprint.)
- **RealDESED: A Real-World Domestic Sound Event Detection Benchmark** (`2607.16736`) — Florian Schmid, Paul Primus, Alexander Fichtinger, Gerhard Widmer (CP-JKU, Johannes Kepler University Linz): 5,710 in-home recordings from 652 participants with temporally precise multi-annotator labels for 15 domestic sound classes plus rich device/scene metadata; a strong transformer baseline reaches PSDS1 0.731, pushing SED past simulated soundscapes toward real deployment.
- **Harness TTS: Towards Context-Aware Expressive Speech Synthesis with Harness Layer** (`2607.17900`) — Shengfan Shen, Xingchen Song, Jian Luan, Shuai Wang (Xiaomi): a lightweight control layer wrapping a TTS engine, reframing style control as closed-set prompt-tool routing with an LLM planner; on CosyVoice3/VoxCPM2 it beats instruction-only control (up to +35.6-pt instruction-following, +0.38 UTMOSv2) with a first tool recommendation in under 50 ms.
- **ESCUCHA: A Spanish Speech Benchmark for Heterogeneous Acoustic Conditions** (`2607.17812`) — Fernando López, Ana Ayala, Jordi Luque (Telefónica): the first Spanish speech-understanding benchmark for large audio-language models — 1,000 human-curated audio questions, 162.9h sourced "from the wild," spanning 9 perceptual + 10 reasoning categories, multiple accents and non-normative speech; SOTA multimodal/speech models trail trained humans by a wide margin.
