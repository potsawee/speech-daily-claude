### 1. ★ Qwen-Music Technical Report — `2607.11699` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 9/10**
*Jin Xu, Kangdi Wang, Ruibin Yuan, Shun Lei, Xize Cheng, Xueyao Zhang, Zhifang Guo, Dake Guo, Lei Xie, **Wei Xue** ★, Yunfei Chu, Zhiyong Wu (27 authors) — Qwen Team, Alibaba Group (with HKUST & Tsinghua SIGS) · cs.SD · 2026-07-13 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.11699)*

*Problem:* End-to-end generation of complete, high-fidelity songs with coherent vocal singing — from text, lyrics, and style — still trails proprietary systems in musicality and structure.
*Method:* A 25 Hz single-codebook semantic tokenizer feeds an autoregressive LLM with a melody-token chain-of-thought that plans melody before full-song synthesis; a generative stereo renderer restores acoustic detail. Trained on 5M hours, post-trained with offline DPO then online GSPO.
*Result:* SOTA on 13/16 objective musicality/quality metrics across 600 EN/ZH prompts; professional listeners prefer it to leading proprietary systems, with more faithful reference-melody preservation for cover songs.
**Why it ranked:** ★ followed author (Wei Xue) and followed lab (Alibaba/Qwen); an ambitious, SOTA full-song system whose semantic-tokenizer + LLM + RL-post-training recipe is interesting well beyond music.

### 2. ★ Qwen-Audio-VAE Technical Report — `2607.11738` · **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Ziyue Jiang, Dake Guo, Zekai Zhang, Hangrui Hu, Ting He, Xinfa Zhu, Xiong Wang, Yongqi Wang, Jiapeng Wang, Wenxiang Guo, Zhifang Guo, Chenfei Wu, Dayiheng Liu, Jin Xu — Qwen Team, Alibaba Group · eess.AS · 2026-07-13 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.11738)*

*Problem:* Scalable general audio generation needs an autoencoder that both reconstructs diverse audio faithfully and produces compact latents fast enough for large-scale text-to-audio training — a balance existing codecs miss.
*Method:* A low-bitrate continuous VAE pairs a causal encoder-decoder with windowed Transformer blocks and multi-discriminator training; an asymmetric backbone plus latency-aware encoder pruning maximizes encoding throughput. Trained on 5M hours of multi-domain audio.
*Result:* Strong reconstruction across public speech, music, and general-sound benchmarks while encoding 32 minutes of audio in just 541 ms — a compact, high-throughput representation backbone for text-to-audio.
**Why it ranked:** ★ followed lab (Alibaba/Qwen) and squarely in the user's core audio-codec/tokenization interest — a scale-trained, unusually fast general-audio VAE built to be a reusable backbone.

### 3. ★ Encoder-Side Neuron Identification and Amplification for Acoustic Perception in Large Audio-Language Models — `2607.11801` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Yu-Han Huang, Chih-Kai Yang, Ke-Han Lu, An-Yu Cheng, **Hung-yi Lee** ★ — National Taiwan University (Speech Processing & Machine Learning Lab) · cs.SD/cs.AI · 2026-07-13 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.11801)*

*Problem:* Large audio-language models handle speech content well but underperform on non-semantic attributes like emotion; existing inference-time fixes act only after the audio encoder, at coarse granularity.
*Method:* IAAN scores every feed-forward neuron inside the audio encoder by contrasting its activation on the real waveform against a noise reference, then amplifies a small set of the highest-scoring "acoustic neurons" at inference — training-free and label-free.
*Result:* Across ten non-semantic attributes, average accuracy rises 25.7 points on Audio-Flamingo-3, 21.4 on Qwen2.5-Omni, and 9.7 on Kimi-Audio; controlled tests show the encoder locus and neuron-level selectivity are both necessary.
**Why it ranked:** ★ followed (Hung-yi Lee) and core audio-LLM territory; a clean, training-free mechanistic intervention with large, carefully-ablated gains across several leading LALMs.

### 4. ★ FdAudio: MeanFlow-Anchored Fréchet-Distance Post-Training for One-Step Text-to-Audio Generation — `2607.10421` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Kuan-Po Huang, Bo-Ru Lu, Ho-Lam Chung, Shih-Hsin Wang, **Hung-yi Lee** ★ — National Taiwan University (with University of Washington) · eess.AS/cs.SD · 2026-07-11 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.10421)*

*Problem:* One-step text-to-audio samplers like MeanAudio are fast but their strict single-step quality lags far behind multi-step diffusion/flow models.
*Method:* FdAudio post-trains the one-step distribution directly with a multi-representation Fréchet-distance loss across pretrained embedding spaces, adding a MeanFlow consistency objective as a structural anchor to prevent the multi-step degradation naive FD post-training causes.
*Result:* SOTA one-step text-to-audio among few-step systems — 11.4% lower FD and 28.8% better FAD than MeanAudio — while a 25-step path still matches or beats strong multi-step models at a fraction of their latency.
**Why it ranked:** ★ followed (Hung-yi Lee) and on-profile (flow-matching audio generation); a targeted post-training fix that lifts one-step T2A quality without the usual multi-step collapse.

### 5. ★ Anysynth: Zero-Shot Instrument Cloning via In-Context Learning and Asymmetric Hierarchical Guidance — `2607.11143` · **Relevance 6/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Chong Jing, Junan Zhang, Jing Yang, Yulun Wu, Fan Fan, **Zhizheng Wu** ★ — The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen (CUHK-Shenzhen) · cs.SD · 2026-07-13 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.11143)*

*Problem:* Zero-shot instrument cloning renders a target MIDI with an unseen instrument's timbre from a short reference, but embedding-based methods compress the reference into a fixed vector, discarding fine-grained acoustic cues.
*Method:* Anysynth is an embedding-free in-context flow-matching synthesizer: a Diffusion Transformer conditions directly on the uncompressed reference audio and target MIDI, letting self-attention retrieve acoustic detail at generation time, with an Asymmetric Hierarchical CFG decoupling MIDI and timbre guidance.
*Result:* Beats embedding-based and autoregressive baselines on audio quality, timbre similarity, and melody adherence, and uniquely shows prompt-length scaling — longer references steadily improve timbre fidelity.
**Why it ranked:** ★ followed (Zhizheng Wu); instrument timbre is niche for this profile, but the embedding-free in-context flow-matching recipe and prompt-length scaling are a genuinely novel, transferable idea for zero-shot cloning.
