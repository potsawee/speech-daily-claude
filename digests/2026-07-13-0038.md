### 1. Flow Matching-Based Speech Source Separation with Best-of-N Biometric Sampling — `2607.06088` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Anastasia Zorkina, Alexandr Anikin, Nikita Khmelev, Anastasiya Korenevskaya, Sergey Novoselov, Vladimir Volokhov, Maxim Korenevsky, Yuriy Matveev (Speech Technology Center / ITMO University, St. Petersburg) · cs.SD · 2026-07-07 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.06088)*

*Problem:* Single-channel speech separation struggles in real deployment — source-permutation ambiguity, generative sampling variability, and chunk-wise processing of long recordings all degrade output.
*Method:* A conditional flow-matching model emits an ordered two-source output conditioned on the mixture; a frozen speaker encoder fixes source order in training and, at inference, drives biometric best-of-N candidate selection plus chunk-level channel alignment.
*Result:* The Transformer U-Net variant matches strong baselines on Libri2Mix (SI-SDR/PESQ/ESTOI) and reaches the lowest downstream ASR cpWER and speaker-verification EER in every setting.
**Why it ranked:** Squarely on-profile (source separation + flow matching) with a genuinely novel biometric best-of-N ordering trick and the best downstream ASR/SV numbers, from an established speaker-recognition group.

### 2. InsideSSL: Understanding Self-Supervised Speech Representations using a Model-Centric Perspective — `2607.06392` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Samir Sadok, Xavier Alameda-Pineda (Inria, Univ. Grenoble Alpes, CNRS, LJK) · cs.SD · 2026-07-07 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.06392)*

*Problem:* SSL speech models (Wav2Vec2, HuBERT, WavLM) underpin most speech tasks, yet their layer-wise internal dynamics remain poorly understood.
*Method:* InsideSSL analyzes each layer through three intrinsic lenses — compression (entropy), geometry (curvature), and robustness to perturbation — and adds a cross-layer Generative Compatibility Matrix for functional transferability, with linear probing linking topology to downstream tasks.
*Result:* Different objectives induce distinct acoustic-compression and manifold-unfolding regimes; the analysis exposes stable phonetic cores, identity volatility, and deep-layer semantic pruning, showing how layer topology dictates phoneme, pitch, and speaker encoding.
**Why it ranked:** A standout model-centric analysis handing over a new interpretability toolkit for the SSL backbones everyone builds on — the quality earns the slot even though analysis sits in the user's secondary interests.

### 3. Why Do You Say It Like That? A Phoneme-Level Framework for Explainable Speech Deepfake Detection — `2607.08586` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Anna Taylor, Michele Panariello, Massimiliano Todisco, Chiara Galdi, Nicholas Evans, Driss Matrouf (EURECOM, Sophia Antipolis; LIA, Avignon Université) · eess.AS/cs.SD · 2026-07-09 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.08586)*

*Problem:* As deepfake detectors get more accurate with SSL features, *why* a clip is judged bona fide or spoofed stays opaque, undermining trust.
*Method:* A post-hoc, phoneme-level explainability framework pairs Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping with speech recognition to produce saliency maps aligned to phonemes and pauses, applicable to any CNN-based detector.
*Result:* On ASVspoof 5 it matches comparable architectures' detection accuracy while surfacing statistically significant, human-readable attack- and speaker-dependent phonetic cues of spoofing.
**Why it ranked:** Directly relevant anti-spoofing work from the EURECOM group behind ASVspoof, trading a little raw accuracy for genuinely interpretable, actionable phonetic explanations.

### 4. PS4: Proxy-Supervised Joint Training for Real Target Speaker Extraction — `2607.08111` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Wanyi Ning, Wei Zhou, Yingpeng Li, Yinshang Guo, Haitao Qian, Yiming Cheng (affiliations not stated on the abstract page; REAL-T / SLT 2026 challenge submission) · cs.SD/cs.AI · 2026-07-09 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.08111)*

*Problem:* Target speaker extraction on real conversational mixtures is bottlenecked by the absence of large corpora and clean target references for supervision.
*Method:* PS4 builds a 71,771-sample Chinese/English corpus (mixtures, per-speaker enrollment, transcripts, frame-level VAD) and fine-tunes only the BSRNN separator of a pretrained model with four differentiable objectives — ASR cross-entropy, speaker similarity, frame-level VAD, and perceptual quality.
*Result:* Ranks 2nd overall on the REAL-T challenge leaderboard, with the best speaker similarity and timing F1 among all submissions.
**Why it ranked:** Core separation/speaker relevance on genuinely real mixtures, a sizable new corpus, and a top-2 leaderboard result — solid despite being a challenge-system paper.

### 5. COALA: Robust Contextualized Speech-augmented Language Modeling for ASR via Contrastive Regularizer and Biasing Score Estimation — `2607.08117` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Jhih-Rong Guo, Bi-Cheng Yan, Tien-Hong Lo, Berlin Chen (National Taiwan Normal University) · cs.CL · 2026-07-09 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.08117)*

*Problem:* Contextual biasing must inject domain entities into ASR, but speech-augmented LMs (SLMs) have limited context windows and collapse during training when many rare words co-occur in one utterance.
*Method:* COALA maps SLM latent representations into a discriminative space to score audio-segment/entity matching — selecting relevant targets from large biasing lists — plus a contrastive regularizer that repairs the multi-target training collapse seen in prior work.
*Result:* On the LibriSpeech benchmark, it consistently improves contextual-biasing performance across a range of biasing-list scales.
**Why it ranked:** On-profile ASR + speech-LM work fixing a real, specific failure (multi-target training collapse) in entity biasing, though evaluation stays LibriSpeech-only.
