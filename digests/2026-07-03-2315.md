### ★ 1. Rethinking Speech-LLM Integration for ASR: Effective Joint Speech-Text Training by Interleaving — `2607.01733` · **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Ruchao Fan, Yiming Wang, Rui Zhao, Liliang Ren, Keqi Deng, Xiaoyang Chen, Ali Zare, Bo Ren, Yuxuan Hu, Junkun Chen, Yan Huang, Yelong Shen, Jinyu Li (Microsoft) · cs.CL / eess.AS · 2026-07-02* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.01733)

*Problem:* Speech-LLM systems promise ASR gains from text pretraining, but as supervised ASR data grows the LLM prior's contribution fades and plain joint speech-text training under-uses textual knowledge.
*Method:* JSTIP builds word- and segment-level interleaved speech-text sequences within aligned pairs for continuous-input speech-LLMs, an ASR-oriented pretraining objective that keeps the LLM's generative prior intact.
*Result:* On 38k hours it consistently improves entity accuracy over ASR-only and joint baselines, matches synthetic-pair adaptation using only domain text, and is competitive with open ASR/Speech-LLM systems on medical entities.
**Why it ranked:** ★ Followed (Jinyu Li, Microsoft) and dead-center on the user's ASR + speech-LLM core — a large-scale, carefully-analyzed study that pins down *when* LLM priors actually help ASR, the kind of grounded result worth sharing.

### ★ 2. An Efficient vLLM-Based Inference Pipeline for Unified Audio Understanding and Generation — `2607.02119` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Haoran Wang, Jinchuan Tian, Siddhant Arora, Shinji Watanabe (Carnegie Mellon University & Shanghai Jiao Tong University) · eess.AS / cs.AI · 2026-07-02* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.02119)

*Problem:* High-throughput inference engines lack native support for multimodal *generation*; speech-LLMs that emit multi-layer audio tokens via AR+NAR or delay-pattern multi-token prediction break vLLM's single-stream loop.
*Method:* They extend autoregressive decoding to natively run delay-pattern de-interleaving and coordinated multi-stream sampling, add an on-GPU acoustic decoder for end-to-end waveform synthesis, and co-schedule paired conditional/unconditional requests to make classifier-free guidance cheap.
*Result:* An open-sourced unified speech understanding+generation pipeline whose CFG sustains ~80% of non-CFG throughput, overturning the assumption that CFG halves it.
**Why it ranked:** ★ Followed (Watanabe, CMU WAVLab) and squarely on spoken-LM infrastructure — a practical, open-sourced serving stack the speech-LLM community actually needs, even though the contribution is engineering rather than a new model.

### ★ 3. Beyond Words: Towards Effective Modeling of Non-Verbal Vocalizations in ASR — `2607.01563` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Gene Yang, Haibin Wu, Peng Su, Ruizhe Huang, Suwon Shon, Bach Do, Minxue Niu, Zhaoheng Ni, Shang-Wen Li, Florian Metze, Yossi Adi, Ming Sun, Yuzong Liu (Meta) · eess.AS · 2026-07-02* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.01563)

*Problem:* ASR transcribes words well but drops nonverbal vocalizations — laughter, breaths, coughs, cries — whose annotations are sparse and heavily long-tailed, starving rare events.
*Method:* Three data-centric strategies: a two-stage curriculum (generic NV token → per-category fine-tuning), inter-token transfer from high-resource events (breath/laughter) to rare ones (crying), and voice-conversion augmentation with class balancing.
*Result:* Shared acoustic structure across vocal events transfers to lift rare-category NV detection while preserving lexical ASR quality.
**Why it ranked:** ★ Followed (Yossi Adi, Meta) and on-profile for ASR + paralinguistics — a credible industry study of a real long-tail gap; capped at Quality 7 because it's a data-centric recipe rather than a new architecture.

### 4. Unlocking Speech-Text Compositional Powers: Instruction-Following Speech Language Models without Instruction Tuning — `2607.02214` · **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Congrui Du, Yang Zhang, Kaizhi Qian, Shiyu Chang (UC Santa Barbara & MIT-IBM Watson AI Lab) · cs.CL / eess.AS · 2026-07-02* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.02214)

*Problem:* Instruction-tuning speech-LLMs is far harder than for text LLMs — it needs a new modality plus speech-specific instructions, and synthesizing massive instruction data doesn't scale as speech sequences are long.
*Method:* SpeechCombine does a single round of continuous speech pretraining (30k hours) on a text-LLM base, then *directly adds* the (instruct − base) text-LLM weight difference to the speech-adapted model — task-arithmetic transfer, no speech instruction tuning at all.
*Result:* The weight combination preserves the text LLM's knowledge and transfers its instruction-following into the speech domain.
**Why it ranked:** The most novel idea in the batch and dead-center on the user's speech-LM core — an elegant weight-arithmetic route to instruction-following SLMs that sidesteps the data-scaling wall, from the AutoVC/SpeechSplit lineage (Qian & Chang).

### 5. Neural Audio Codec with Adjustable Token Temporal Resolution Using Sampling-Frequency-Independent Convolutional Layers — `2607.01865` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Tomohiko Nakamura (AIST), Wataru Nakata, Kanami Imamura, Yuki Saito (University of Tokyo, Saruwatari–Saito Lab), Japan · eess.AS / cs.SD · 2026-07-02 · IWAENC 2026* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.01865)

*Problem:* A neural audio codec's token temporal resolution (TTR) trades off capturing fast acoustic events against sequence length, yet most codecs fix one TTR and need retraining for each.
*Method:* Treating TTR as the token sequence's sampling period, sampling-frequency-independent convolutional layers generate TTR-dependent kernels from one shared parameter set, adjusting kernel size and stride per TTR — dropped into Descript Audio Codec with the quantizer unchanged.
*Result:* A single codec serving multiple TTRs beats a baseline that swaps in TTR-specific layers, on environmental-sound reconstruction.
**Why it ranked:** Neural codecs / audio tokenizers are an explicit core interest; this is a clean, genuinely novel mechanism (one codec, many token rates) that's directly useful for token-based audio LMs — capped at Quality 7 as a focused, workshop-scale result evaluated only on environmental sound.
