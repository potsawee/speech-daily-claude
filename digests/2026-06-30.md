## Top 5

### 1. DTM-Codec: Dynamic Token Masking for VFR Speech Coding — `2606.29480` · score 8.3
**Authors:** Hoyeol Sohn, Juhan Nam (KAIST) · `cs.SD, eess.AS` · Jun 28
A neural speech codec that keeps selected encoder tokens and learns to fill masked positions, showing clear gains over fixed-frame-rate baselines under a **strict matched-total-bitrate** protocol (counting VFR side-information overhead honestly).
**Why it ranked:** Neural codec / speech tokenizer — your highest-priority cluster — with a rigorous fairness protocol that addresses a real weakness in prior VFR work.

### 2. Preference-ASR: A Preference-Aware Test Set for Benchmarking ASR in the Era of Speech LLMs — `2606.29534` · score 8.0
**Authors:** Koluguri, Meister, Karpov, Zelasko, Raj, Balam (NVIDIA NeMo) · `eess.AS, cs.CL` · Jun 28
A benchmark that measures whether ASR systems **follow natural-language preference instructions** (normalization, entities, disfluencies, casing) — distinctions standard normalizers erase — built from 7 corpora via an LLM-assisted, human-verified pipeline.
**Why it ranked:** Core ASR + speech-LLM evaluation from a top industrial ASR group; reframes ASR scoring for the instruction-following era.

### 3. OLIVE: View-Augmented Latent Prediction with Waveform Reconstruction for Speech SSL — `2606.30356` · score 7.9
**Authors:** Karl El Hajal, Mathew Magimai-Doss (Idiap) · cross-listed `cs.SD, eess.AS, cs.CL, cs.LG` · Jun 29
A self-supervised speech framework jointly optimizing **masked latent prediction + waveform reconstruction**, so early features keep signal detail while later layers become invariant for robust downstream tasks.
**Why it ranked:** Foundational SSL with a clean analysis-plus-synthesis objective; cross-listing in all four categories signals broad relevance.

### 4. VIB-AVSR: Variational Information Bottleneck for Noise-Robust LLM-Based Audio-Visual Speech Recognition — `2606.29632` · score 7.8
**Authors:** Arora, Singh, Cappellazzo, Petridis, Pantic (Imperial) · `cs.SD, eess.AS` · Jun 28
Adds an explicit information-bottleneck mechanism so an LLM-based AVSR backbone produces **stable representations under corrupted audio**, targeting the noise-robustness gap in clean-optimized AVSR-LLMs.
**Why it ranked:** ASR + speech-LLM + multimodal robustness from a leading AVSR lab — a core topic with a concrete novel mechanism.

### 5. TF-MoE: Time-Frequency Mixture-of-Experts for Efficient Speech Separation — `2606.29575` · score 7.6
**Authors:** Hu, Li, Zhang, S. Liu, Lu, Qian (SJTU / Microsoft) · `cs.SD` · Jun 28
A sparse MoE over a mel-band Conformer with **alternating time-wise and frequency-wise experts** (selected per frame / per mel band), raising capacity at almost no inference-cost increase for edge deployment.
**Why it ranked:** Source separation + novel efficient architecture + deployability — core interest with a strong efficiency angle.
