## Top 5

### 1. Preference-ASR: Benchmarking ASR in the Era of Speech LLMs — `2606.29534` · **Relevance 8.0/10 · Quality 8.5/10**
**Authors:** Koluguri, Meister, Karpov, Zelasko, Raj, Balam — **NVIDIA (NeMo)** · `eess.AS, cs.CL` · Jun 28

*Problem:* ASR test sets use inconsistent formatting and standard normalizers erase it, so benchmarks can't tell whether a model honors a requested output style.
*Method:* PreferenceASR scores whether systems follow natural-language preference instructions across normalization, entities, disfluencies, and case, built from seven corpora via an LLM-assisted, human-verified pipeline.
*Result:* A benchmark that reframes ASR evaluation for the speech-LLM era, where models should be instructable rather than emit one canonical transcript.

**Why it ranked:** Highly relevant ASR×speech-LLM evaluation, and high quality — a carefully built NVIDIA NeMo benchmark of this kind tends to get adopted and shape how the field reports results.

### 2. DTM-Codec: Dynamic Token Masking for VFR Speech Coding — `2606.29480` · **Relevance 8.3/10 · Quality 7.5/10**
**Authors:** Hoyeol Sohn, Juhan Nam — **KAIST** · `cs.SD, eess.AS` · Jun 28

*Problem:* Variable-frame-rate neural codecs save bits by allocating frames unevenly, but must also transmit which frames they kept — overhead that often cancels the savings.
*Method:* Dynamic Token Masking keeps a subset of encoder tokens and trains the decoder to fill masked positions from a learned token, with efficient boundary selection.
*Result:* Clear quality gains over fixed-frame-rate baselines under a strict bitrate protocol that counts the side-information overhead prior VFR work often ignored.

**Why it ranked:** Top-relevance codec/tokenizer work, and solid quality — a focused KAIST contribution with an unusually honest matched-bitrate evaluation that fixes a real VFR weakness.

### 3. VIB-AVSR: Variational Information Bottleneck for Noise-Robust LLM-Based AVSR — `2606.29632` · **Relevance 7.8/10 · Quality 7.5/10**
**Authors:** Arora, Singh, Cappellazzo, Petridis, Pantic — **Imperial College London** · `cs.SD, eess.AS` · Jun 28

*Problem:* Audio-visual ASR uses lips when audio is noisy, but recent LLM-backbone systems are tuned for clean audio with no mechanism for stability under corrupted audio.
*Method:* Insert a Variational Information Bottleneck into the AVSR-LLM pipeline to squeeze out noise-sensitive information and encourage stable representations under degraded audio.
*Result:* Targets the robustness gap LLM-based AVSR models show in noise, without sacrificing their strong clean-condition accuracy.

**Why it ranked:** Core ASR×speech-LLM robustness from a leading AVSR lab (Imperial); the principled VIB mechanism gives it credibility beyond a bolt-on tweak.

### 4. OLIVE: View-Augmented Latent Prediction with Waveform Reconstruction for Speech SSL — `2606.30356` · **Relevance 7.9/10 · Quality 7.0/10**
**Authors:** Karl El Hajal, Mathew Magimai-Doss — **Idiap Research Institute** · cross-listed `cs.SD/eess.AS/cs.CL/cs.LG` · Jun 29

*Problem:* Self-supervised speech encoders usually optimize either masked latent prediction or waveform reconstruction, rarely capturing both semantic context and signal-level fidelity at once.
*Method:* OLIVE combines view-augmented masked latent prediction with reconstruction under one objective, so early layers retain signal detail while later layers become invariant and robust.
*Result:* A single representation supporting a broad span of downstream tasks; cross-listing across all four arXiv categories signals unusually broad relevance.

**Why it ranked:** Very relevant SSL building block with a clean analysis-plus-synthesis idea; quality is good, though SSL is crowded so its ultimate impact is less certain.

### 5. TF-MoE: Time-Frequency Mixture-of-Experts for Efficient Speech Separation — `2606.29575` · **Relevance 7.6/10 · Quality 7.0/10**
**Authors:** Hu, Li, Zhang, S. Liu, Lu, Qian — **SJTU / Microsoft** · `cs.SD` · Jun 28

*Problem:* Compact speech-separation models have shrunk in parameters, but their compute cost still blocks deployment on edge devices.
*Method:* A sparse Mixture-of-Experts on a mel-band Conformer alternates time-wise and frequency-wise modules, routing each frame and mel band to specialized experts so few fire per step.
*Result:* Higher capacity and specialization at inference cost — and latency — close to the dense baseline.

**Why it ranked:** Relevant, deployable separation work from strong groups; a sensible efficiency contribution in a mature area rather than a breakthrough.
