## Top 5

### 1. ★ GigaSpeechBench: A Real-World Multilingual Speech-to-Text Benchmark — `2606.28884` · **Relevance 8.5/10 · Quality 8.5/10**
**Authors:** Tu, Yang, Wang, ... Kai Yu, Zhifu Gao, Xiangang Li, Xie Chen (35 authors) — **SJTU / NPU / Fudan / NTU Singapore consortium** · `eess.AS` · Jun 27

*Problem:* ASR benchmarks overstate real-world robustness because they skip domain terms, age variation, dialects, accents, and low-resource languages across the Middle East and Southeast Asia.
*Method:* 680 hours of human-annotated multilingual ASR/AST data spanning five modules: 12 low-resource languages plus Japanese/Korean, 6 Chinese dialects, 6 English accents, dense-terminology domains, and elderly/child speech, with translations for AST.
*Result:* Leading foundation models and commercial APIs show significant degradation, exposing evaluation blind spots high-resource benchmarks hide.

**Why it ranked:** Core ASR-evaluation relevance plus standout quality — a large multi-institution consortium benchmark (★ Kai Yu & Xie Chen among 35 authors) that exposes coverage gaps current systems hide; likely to become a reference eval.

### 2. AudioCALM: Continuous Autoregressive Language Modeling for Universal Audio Generation — `2606.23080` · **Relevance 8.3/10 · Quality 8.0/10**
**Authors:** Liu, Luo, Wang, Chen, Ma, Li, Xue — **HKUST / Alibaba** · `eess.AS, cs.SD` · Jun 22

*Problem:* Unifying speech, sound, and music generation in one model is blocked by fidelity/conditioning trade-offs; naive joint training disproportionately hurts sound and music when mixed with tightly time-aligned speech.
*Method:* Extends autoregressive next-token prediction to continuous latents via a flow-matching head and block-causal attention, plus an Asymmetric Mixture-of-Modality-Experts giving speech a dedicated residual expert.
*Result:* Matches modality-specific SOTA and beats prior unified baselines across speech, sound, and music generation.

**Why it ranked:** Not a followed author, but genuine architectural novelty (A-MoME) and SOTA across three distinct generation tasks earns the slot on quality alone — exactly the asymmetric case the ranking rule allows for.

### 3. ★ Interleaved Speech Language Models Latently Work In Text — `2606.22473` · **Relevance 8.0/10 · Quality 8.0/10**
**Authors:** Sternberg, Maimon, Adi — **Hebrew University of Jerusalem** · `cs.CL, cs.LG, cs.SD, eess.AS` · Jun 21

*Problem:* It's unclear how speech and text tokens actually interact inside interleaved speech-text LMs, despite the paradigm being used to boost speech-only capability.
*Method:* Logit-lens analysis across several interleaved SLM families and sizes tracks how representations evolve layer by layer during speech processing.
*Result:* Models pass through an implicit transcription phase — the spoken word's text token is a top decodable candidate in up to 77% of cases — before predicting the next word in text space and converting back to speech.

**Why it ranked:** A ★ followed-author (Yossi Adi) mechanistic-interpretability paper on a core speech-LM question — the kind of field-shaping insight worth sharing even without a new SOTA number.

### 4. ★ VoiceTTA: Enhancing Zero-Shot TTS via RL-Based Test-Time Adaptation — `2606.26534` · **Relevance 8.0/10 · Quality 7.5/10**
**Authors:** Xie, Li, Yu, Liu — **Tencent AI Lab / HKUST(GZ)** · `cs.SD, cs.AI` · Jun 25

*Problem:* Zero-shot TTS struggles to imitate unseen speaking styles such as crosstalk or dialects, and fine-tuning for personalization needs large, high-quality data.
*Method:* Reinforcement-learning test-time adaptation optimizes learnable prefixes in a flow-matching TTS model via GRPO, with F0/energy style rewards plus speaker-similarity and Whisper-WER intelligibility rewards.
*Result:* Substantial gains on uncommon speech prompts, beating SOTA baselines with no retraining.

**Why it ranked:** ★ Followed author (Dong Yu) tackling a real zero-shot TTS weakness with a well-motivated RL recipe and clean evaluation gains — solid rather than groundbreaking, but clearly relevant.

### 5. ★ LeVo 2: Stable and Melodious Song Generation via Hierarchical Representation Modeling — `2606.30642` · **Relevance 7.0/10 · Quality 8.0/10**
**Authors:** Lei, Zhang, Wu, Xu, ... Wu, Yu (11 authors) — **Tencent AI Lab / Tsinghua SIGS** · `cs.SD, cs.AI` · Jun 29

*Problem:* Full-length song generation must balance vocal-instrument coordination against track-specific acoustic detail, a trade-off prior LM-based systems resolve poorly.
*Method:* A hybrid LLM-diffusion system predicts mixed tokens for semantic planning, then vocal/accompaniment tokens in parallel for refinement, trained via an aesthetics-guided progressive schedule (SFT, offline DPO, semi-online DPO).
*Result:* Beats open-source baselines on six subjective dimensions and approaches commercial systems on several listening metrics.

**Why it ranked:** ★ Followed author (Dong Yu); a secondary-interest topic (music generation) but standout execution and unusually thorough evaluation justify the slot over more incremental core-ASR/TTS work.

### Also notable (just outside top 5)
- **★ ESPnet3** (`2606.21854`, Watanabe et al.): infrastructure paper for the next-gen ESPnet — faster training, better GPU utilization; high practical value, lower research novelty.
- **CTC-Seeded Token Edit Refinement** (`2606.28732`, Huang & Wang): treats non-autoregressive ASR decoding as edit refinement of a CTC hypothesis — just 2 edit steps give substantial WER gains, a genuinely novel decoding paradigm.
- **HPRO** (`2606.28249`): hierarchical preference-optimization framework fixing reward-hacking and scale-mismatch issues in emotional TTS RLHF.
- **★ SpeechEQ** (`2606.25990`, Huck Yang et al.): a 2,265-dialogue benchmark for emotional-intelligence reasoning in voice conversational models.
- **★ CAAD** (`2606.23052`, Hung-Yi Lee): distills contrastive audio-grounding into speech-LM weights, ~8% gain over standard distillation without inference-time overhead.
