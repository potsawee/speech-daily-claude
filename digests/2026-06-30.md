## Top 5

### 1. DTM-Codec: Dynamic Token Masking for VFR Speech Coding — `2606.29480` · score 8.3
**Authors:** Hoyeol Sohn, Juhan Nam — **KAIST** · `cs.SD, eess.AS` · Jun 28
Variable-frame-rate (VFR) neural codecs save bits by spending fewer frames on steady speech and more on fast-changing regions, but they also have to signal *which* frames they kept — overhead that often cancels the savings. DTM-Codec introduces Dynamic Token Masking: it retains a chosen subset of encoder tokens, trains the decoder to reconstruct the masked positions from a learned fill token, and adds an efficient boundary-selection method for picking which tokens to keep. Its headline result is clear quality gains over fixed-frame-rate baselines even under a strict protocol that counts the side-information bits in the total bitrate — a fairness check prior VFR work often skipped.
**Why it ranked:** Neural codec / speech tokenizer — your top-priority cluster — with an honest evaluation that fixes a real weakness in earlier VFR codecs.

### 2. Preference-ASR: A Preference-Aware Test Set for Benchmarking ASR in the Era of Speech LLMs — `2606.29534` · score 8.0
**Authors:** Koluguri, Meister, Karpov, Zelasko, Raj, Balam — **NVIDIA (NeMo)** · `eess.AS, cs.CL` · Jun 28
Popular ASR test sets use inconsistent conventions for numbers, entities, disfluencies, and casing, and standard normalizers erase exactly those formatting distinctions — so existing benchmarks can't tell whether a model honors a requested output style. PreferenceASR is a test set that measures whether ASR systems follow natural-language *preference instructions* across four categories (normalization, entities, disfluencies, case), built from seven open-source corpora through a two-stage LLM-assisted pipeline with human verification. It reframes ASR evaluation for the speech-LLM era, where models are expected to be instructable rather than emit a single canonical transcript.
**Why it ranked:** Core ASR + speech-LLM evaluation from a leading industrial ASR group; redefines what "accuracy" means once the model is promptable.

### 3. OLIVE: View-Augmented Latent Prediction with Waveform Reconstruction for Speech SSL — `2606.30356` · score 7.9
**Authors:** Karl El Hajal, Mathew Magimai-Doss — **Idiap Research Institute** · cross-listed `cs.SD/eess.AS/cs.CL/cs.LG` · Jun 29
Self-supervised speech encoders usually optimize *either* masked latent prediction (strong contextual/semantic features) *or* waveform reconstruction (strong signal fidelity), rarely both at once. OLIVE unifies them under a single objective — view-augmented masked latent prediction plus reconstruction — so reconstruction forces the early encoder layers to keep signal-level detail while masked prediction pushes the later layers toward invariant, noise-robust representations. The single resulting representation is shown to support a broad span of downstream tasks, and the paper's cross-listing across all four arXiv categories signals unusually broad relevance.
**Why it ranked:** Foundational SSL with a clean analysis-plus-synthesis objective — a building block that feeds TTS, ASR, and codecs alike.

### 4. VIB-AVSR: Variational Information Bottleneck for Noise-Robust LLM-Based Audio-Visual Speech Recognition — `2606.29632` · score 7.8
**Authors:** Arora, Singh, Cappellazzo, Petridis, Pantic — **Imperial College London** · `cs.SD, eess.AS` · Jun 28
Audio-visual speech recognition leans on lip movements when audio is noisy, and recent systems bolt pretrained AV encoders onto an LLM backbone — but those are tuned mostly for clean audio, with no explicit mechanism to keep the LLM's representations stable when the acoustic stream is corrupted. VIB-AVSR inserts a Variational Information Bottleneck that squeezes out noise-sensitive information, encouraging stable features under degraded audio. The goal is to close the robustness gap that LLM-based AVSR models exhibit in noise, without giving up their strong clean-condition accuracy.
**Why it ranked:** ASR + speech-LLM + multimodal robustness from a top AVSR lab — a core topic with a concrete, principled mechanism.

### 5. TF-MoE: Time-Frequency Mixture-of-Experts for Efficient Speech Separation — `2606.29575` · score 7.6
**Authors:** Hu, Li, Zhang, S. Liu, Lu, Qian — **SJTU / Microsoft** · `cs.SD` · Jun 28
Compact speech-separation front-ends have shrunk in parameter count, but their compute cost still blocks deployment on edge devices. TF-MoE adds capacity almost for free using a sparse Mixture-of-Experts on a mel-band-splitting Conformer: it alternates time-wise and frequency-wise MoE modules that dynamically route each time frame, and each mel band, to specialized experts, so only a few experts activate per step. This raises model capacity and specialization while keeping inference cost — and therefore latency — close to the dense baseline.
**Why it ranked:** Source separation + a novel efficiency-oriented architecture (sparse TF-MoE) aimed squarely at deployability.
