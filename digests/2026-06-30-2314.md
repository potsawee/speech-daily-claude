### 1. ★ Comparative Reasoning: Making an Audio Language Model Better at Comparing Emotions — `2606.24082` · **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Abinay Reddy Naini, Jaeyeon Kim, **Chao-Han Huck Yang**, **Shinji Watanabe**, Carlos Busso (UT Dallas, CMU, NVIDIA) · eess.AS/cs.SD · 2026-06-23*

*Problem:* LALMs can reason about audio, but it's unclear if they can compare two speech signals — emotion, prosody, etc. — rather than just describe one in isolation.
*Method:* A reasoning-guided ordinal SER framework conditions an LALM on paired speech, trained on reasoning traces from semantic descriptions plus GeMAPS acoustic evidence, then sharpened with DPO for emotional separation.
*Result:* Improves comparative emotion-preference prediction using only 5% of the data conventional ordinal SER systems require.
**Why it ranked:** Two followed authors (Chao-Han Huck Yang, Shinji Watanabe) on a core audio-LLM topic, with a data-efficient, DPO-refined method and clean ablations.

### 2. ★ VoiceTTA: Enhancing Zero-Shot Text-to-Speech via Reinforcement Learning-Based Test-Time Adaptation — `2606.26534` · **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Tianxin Xie, Chenxing Li, **Dong Yu**, Li Liu (Tencent AI Lab, HKUST(GZ)) · cs.SD/cs.AI · 2026-06-25*

*Problem:* Zero-shot TTS often fails to imitate unseen, uncommon speaking styles (crosstalk, dialects), and fine-tuning for them needs large datasets that block rapid personalization.
*Method:* VoiceTTA runs RL-based test-time adaptation on a flow-matching TTS model, optimizing learnable prefixes via GRPO against style rewards (F0/energy variation), speaker similarity, and Whisper-measured intelligibility.
*Result:* Substantially improves voice imitation on uncommon prompts, beating state-of-the-art baselines with no extra fine-tuning data.
**Why it ranked:** A genuinely new RL-at-inference-time angle on a core zero-shot TTS problem, co-authored by followed researcher Dong Yu at Tencent AI Lab.

### 3. Real-Time Voice AI Hears but Does Not Listen — `2606.26083` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 9/10**
*Martijn Bartelds, Federico Bianchi, James Zou (Stanford) · cs.CL/eess.AS · 2026-06-24*

*Problem:* It's unclear whether realtime voice assistants act on vocal tone or just the transcribed words when the two convey conflicting information.
*Method:* Four production systems (GPT Realtime 2, Gemini Live, Qwen3.5 Omni) are tested on calls where distress, fear, or sarcasm contradicts the literal words.
*Result:* All four ignore the voice and act on the transcript — ending calls with crying callers, approving frightened wire transfers, missing sarcasm — despite being able to name the emotion when asked directly.
**Why it ranked:** Not tied to a followed author or lab, but the single highest quality score among today's 160 candidates — a rigorous, surprising safety finding about deployed commercial voice AI, worth surfacing even at moderate topical relevance.

### 4. AudioCALM: Continuous Autoregressive Language Modeling for Universal Audio Generation — `2606.23080` · **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Huadai Liu, Kaicheng Luo, Wen Wang, Qian Chen, Bin Ma, Xiangang Li, Wei Xue (Alibaba, HKUST) · eess.AS/cs.SD · 2026-06-22*

*Problem:* Unifying speech, sound, and music generation in one model is blocked by tradeoffs between fidelity, end-to-end training, in-context conditioning, and variable-length synthesis.
*Method:* AudioCALM extends autoregressive next-token prediction to continuous audio latents via a flow-matching head, with an Asymmetric Mixture-of-Modality-Experts giving speech a dedicated residual expert while sound and music share the backbone.
*Result:* Matches modality-specific SOTA and beats prior unified baselines across speech, sound, and music generation, with no inference overhead for non-speech inputs.
**Why it ranked:** An ambitious, technically careful unification attempt with a real architectural fix — not just scale — for the cross-modal text-audio mismatch that has blocked prior unified models.

### 5. CTC-Seeded Token Edit Refinement for Non-Autoregressive Speech Recognition — `2606.28732` · **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Wanting Huang, Weiran Wang · eess.AS · 2026-06-27*

*Problem:* Non-autoregressive ASR refinement methods typically start from masked or fixed-length sequences, needing many iterations to reconstruct a full transcript.
*Method:* Reformulates decoding as variable-length edit refinement of a greedy CTC hypothesis — an Edit Flow decoder predicts insertions, deletions, and substitutions in parallel, trained jointly with CTC via a continuous-time discrete diffusion loss.
*Result:* Just two edit steps yield large WER reductions, further sharpened by classifier-free guidance and CTC-confidence-constrained edits.
**Why it ranked:** A genuinely new decoding paradigm for non-autoregressive ASR — edit refinement of a CTC seed, not another masked-refinement variant — with strong ablations and only 2 inference steps needed.
