### 1. ★ Interleaved Speech Language Models Latently Work In Text — `2606.22473` · **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Talia Sternberg, Gallil Maimon, **Yossi Adi** (Hebrew University of Jerusalem; Adi also Meta AI) · cs.CL/cs.LG/cs.SD/eess.AS · 2026-06-21*

*Problem:* Interleaved speech-text LMs handle both modalities well, but how they internally bridge audio and text is opaque.
*Method:* Logit-lens analysis across layers traces where spoken-word information becomes text-decodable, and studies how interleaving and text-LM initialization enable it.
*Result:* Models latently transcribe — the correct text token surfaces in intermediate layers for ~77% of words without any ASR training — then predict the next word in text space before returning to speech.
**Why it ranked:** Core spoken-LM topic from followed author Yossi Adi, with a crisp, rigorous mechanistic insight (implicit transcription) that reframes how we read what speech LMs are doing inside.

### 2. ★ GigaSpeechBench: A Real-World Multilingual Speech-to-Text Benchmark — `2606.28884` · **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Yujie Tu, …, **Xie Chen**, **Kai Yu** (SJTU), Lei Xie (NWPU), Eng-Siong Chng (NTU), Mark Hasegawa-Johnson (UIUC), Xipeng Qiu (Fudan), + SpeechColab/GigaSpeech consortium · eess.AS · 2026-06-27*

*Problem:* ASR looks solved on high-resource benchmarks, but those scores overstate real-world robustness on dialects, accents, terminology, age variation, and low-resource languages.
*Method:* Curate 680 hours of human-annotated in-the-wild speech across five modules — 12 low-resource Middle-Eastern/SE-Asian languages, 6 Chinese dialects, 6 English accents, dense terminology in 12 domains, and child/older-adult speech — plus human translations for AST.
*Result:* Leading foundation models and commercial APIs degrade sharply, exposing blind spots for a billion-plus under-evaluated speakers.
**Why it ranked:** Squarely on the user's ASR/multilingual focus, and a large SJTU-led consortium (followed authors Xie Chen, Kai Yu) makes it an authoritative, immediately useful benchmark.

### 3. ★ LeVo 2: Stable and Melodious Song Generation via Hierarchical Representation Modeling — `2606.30642` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Shun Lei, Jianwei Yu, **Dong Yu** (Tencent AI Lab), Zhiyong Wu (Tsinghua), et al. · cs.SD/cs.AI · 2026-06-29*

*Problem:* Full-length song generation forces a trade-off: mixed-token LMs keep vocals and accompaniment coordinated but blur track detail, while dual-track prediction sharpens acoustics yet weakens global planning.
*Method:* A hybrid LLM-Diffusion framework hierarchically predicts mixed tokens for semantic planning, then parallel vocal/accompaniment tokens, with a diffusion music codec and progressive, aesthetics-guided SFT → offline/semi-online DPO alignment.
*Result:* Outperforms open-source baselines across six subjective dimensions and approaches leading commercial systems on several listening metrics.
**Why it ranked:** Music generation is only a secondary interest, but the ambition, hierarchical design, and preference-aligned training from Tencent AI Lab (followed author Dong Yu) make it a standout-quality share.

### 4. One Model, Many Latencies: Universal Speech Enhancement for Diverse Real-Time Applications — `2606.25621` · **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Szu-Wei Fu, Xuesong Yang, Ante Jukić, Yu-Chiang Frank Wang (NVIDIA), Yu Tsao (Academia Sinica), et al. · cs.SD · 2026-06-24*

*Problem:* Real-time speech enhancement usually needs a separately trained model per latency budget, which is costly to maintain across applications.
*Method:* A single universal model with explicit control of algorithmic latency (adjustable look-ahead frames) and compute latency (early-exit), using parallel convolutions for different look-aheads and a two-stage shared-to-multiple decoder transition.
*Result:* One model deploys across latency settings without retraining, narrowing the gap to specialized models; weights are released on Hugging Face.
**Why it ranked:** Hits core speech enhancement plus the real-time/streaming priority signal, and a practical, deployable NVIDIA/Academia Sinica system with released weights clears the quality bar.

### 5. Preference-ASR: A Preference-Aware Test Set for Benchmarking ASR in the Era of Speech LLMs — `2606.29534` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Nithin Rao Koluguri, Piotr Żelasko, Desh Raj, Jagadeesh Balam, Boris Ginsburg (NVIDIA NeMo) · cs.CL/eess.AS · 2026-06-28*

*Problem:* ASR benchmarks use inconsistent conventions for numbers, disfluencies, entities, and casing, and standard normalizers erase the formatting users actually care about — so preference-following can't be measured.
*Method:* Build PreferenceASR from seven open corpora via a two-stage, LLM-assisted, human-verified pipeline, evaluating models on natural-language preference instructions across four categories with a preference-aware normalizer.
*Result:* Model rankings shift across preference types, revealing quality differences that traditional WER evaluation hides; the dataset is released.
**Why it ranked:** A timely, well-executed evaluation gap for the speech-LLM era from the credible NVIDIA NeMo team, squarely in the user's ASR wheelhouse.
