### ★ 1. LMPAN: A Lightweight Multi-Path Alignment Network for Joint Full-Duplex Acoustic Echo Cancellation and Noise Suppression — `2607.02062` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Chengwei Liu, Shaofei Xue, Haoyin Yan, Xiaotao Liang, Zheng Xue (Alibaba — Qwen Business Unit & Tongyi AI Lab) · eess.AS · 2026-07-02 · Interspeech 2026* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.02062)

*Problem:* On-device full-duplex dialogue needs joint echo cancellation and noise suppression, but hardware distortions, temporal/energy mismatches, and dynamic acoustics degrade quality at tiny compute budgets.
*Method:* LMPAN aligns reference, linear-AEC, and microphone signals across multiple paths, fuses them with scenario-adaptive attention, and adds an SSL-trained post-filter that produces dynamic targets for downstream ASR/VAD.
*Result:* With just 480K parameters and 126 MACs it matches far heavier joint AEC+NS systems.
**Why it ranked:** ★ Followed lab (Alibaba Qwen/Tongyi) and dead-center on the user's speech-enhancement core, made timely by the full-duplex voice-AI wave — a practical, genuinely tiny on-device stack; capped at Quality 7 as careful engineering rather than a new paradigm.

### 2. H-SAGE: Holistic Speaker-Aware Guided Experts for MoE-based Multi-Talker ASR — `2607.01566` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Yujie Guo, Jiaming Zhou, Yuhang Jia, Yang Chen, Yong Qin (Nankai University — NKU-HLT) · cs.SD · 2026-07-02* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.01566)

*Problem:* Multi-talker ASR struggles under heavy speech overlap; existing MoE approaches route frames independently (temporal myopia) and learn expert specialization only implicitly from the ASR loss.
*Method:* H-SAGE adds a Speaker-Aware Global Encoder for long-range context supervised by an Overlap-Aware Loss, plus a Holistic Gating Mechanism that arbitrates expert selection using both global context and local detail.
*Result:* Explicit speaker/overlap grounding improves expert routing and transcription accuracy under high-overlap multi-talker conditions.
**Why it ranked:** Squarely on the user's ASR core and a genuinely novel fix to a real MoE-routing weakness (frame-myopic, ungrounded experts); Quality 7 — credible Nankai speech group and a well-motivated design, though no venue/affiliation is stated in the metadata (inferred from the NKU-HLT code repo).

### 3. Adapting Foundation ASR Models to Dysarthric Speech: A Case Study — `2606.31722` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Christian Huber, Laura Kernahan, Alexander Waibel (Karlsruhe Institute of Technology — Interactive Systems Lab) · cs.CL · 2026-06-30* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.31722)

*Problem:* Foundation ASR models transcribe dysarthric speech poorly, limiting everyday communication for affected speakers.
*Method:* Personalize Whisper to a single dysarthric speaker with 92h of read speech plus 8.8h of in-app user corrections; compare full fine-tuning against LoRA and a Qwen3-ASR base.
*Result:* Fine-tuning cut WER to 15.8% with 1.4h of data, 10.7% with 22.5h, and a best 9.7% using everything; LoRA and Qwen3-ASR did worse.
**Why it ranked:** ASR + accessibility hits the user's core, and it's a credible, honest case study from Waibel's lab — including the negative finding that LoRA and a newer foundation model underperformed full fine-tuning here; a recipe/case-study rather than a new method, so Quality 7.

### 4. Positive-Incentive Noise Predictor for Adversarial Purification in Speaker Verification — `2607.00899` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Yibo Bai, Michele Panariello, Massimiliano Todisco, Nicholas Evans (EURECOM — Audio Security & Privacy Group); Hao Ma, Xiao-Lei Zhang, Xuelong Li (Northwestern Polytechnical University / TeleAI); Sizhou Chen (Univ. of Sydney) · eess.AS · 2026-07-01 · submitted to IEEE TASLP* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.00899)

*Problem:* Speaker-verification systems are vulnerable to adversarial perturbations; diffusion-based purification helps but its iterative reverse denoising is too slow for deployment.
*Method:* Observing that the forward noising step supplies most of the robustness, PnP recasts purification as a learnable "positive-incentive" noise predictor that generates input-adaptive noise mixed into the signal — no reverse sampling.
*Result:* Across four ASV backbones it defends against adversarial attacks while preserving accuracy on clean speech.
**Why it ranked:** Speaker verification + robustness is a listed core interest, and the π-noise reformulation (skip the expensive reverse diffusion, keep the gains) is a clean, well-scoped idea from EURECOM's ASVspoof lineage — a solid TASLP-track contribution, hence Quality 7.

### 5. MeloDISinger: Melody-Aware & Duration-Preserving Singing Voice Editing with Audio Infilling — `2606.30580` · **Relevance 6/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Yoonjeong Park, Jaekwon Im, Juhan Nam (KAIST — Music & Audio Computing Lab) · eess.AS / cs.SD · 2026-06-29 · Interspeech 2026* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.30580)

*Problem:* Text-based singing-voice editing must revise sung lyrics while preserving the original melody, total duration, and untouched regions — hard for existing methods.
*Method:* A flow-matching editor whose MeloDRP module predicts fixed-budget, span-wise duration ratios by fusing phonetic cues with pseudo-MIDI melody via cross-attention, then a flow-matching mel decoder infills the edited spans in context.
*Result:* State-of-the-art melody-aware, duration-preserving singing edits, plus an LLM+WhisperX pipeline for building feasible evaluation cases.
**Why it ranked:** Singing-voice generation is secondary to the TTS/ASR core, but flow-matching for audio is an explicit interest and this is a clean, novel contribution (span-wise duration control + audio infilling) from KAIST's MAC lab with a SOTA claim and an Interspeech 2026 slot — earns its place on quality.
