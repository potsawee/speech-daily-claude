### 1. Probing-Guided Layer Selection from Self-Supervised Speech Models for Generalizable Audio Deepfake Detection — `2606.30791` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 8/10**
Marjan Beheshti, Majid Rostami, Bo Chen — affiliation not listed on the preprint · cs.SD, eess.AS · 2026-06-29 · *Submitted to Computer Speech & Language* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.30791)
*Problem:* Audio deepfake detectors overfit to specific attacks and generalize poorly; SSL-based ones use a single layer or fuse all indiscriminately, exposing layer importance only after training.
*Method:* A model-agnostic two-stage recipe — lightweight XGBoost probes rank each frozen transformer layer's cross-domain discriminative power, then a compact classifier fuses only the top layers via per-layer attention pooling.
*Result:* On XLS-R-300M, 4 selected layers (1.34M params) reach 4.94% EER In-The-Wild — a 28% relative gain over the best prior frozen-backbone system; informative layers cluster in depth "zones."
**Why it ranked:** Anti-spoofing is a core speaker-security interest and this is the day's strongest paper — clear cross-domain SOTA with a tiny trainable head, a clean model-agnostic recipe, and a genuinely informative finding about which SSL layers carry the signal.

### 2. Improving Multichannel Speech Enhancement through Accurate Room-Acoustic Simulations — `2606.31552` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7/10**
Georg Götz, Alessia Milo, Steinar Guðjónsson, Daniel Gert Nielsen, Jesper Pedersen, Finnur Pind — Treble Technologies · eess.AS, cs.AI, cs.LG, cs.SD · 2026-06-30 · *Accepted at Interspeech 2026* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.31552)
*Problem:* Deep multichannel speech-enhancement training relies on room-acoustic simulation, but most pipelines use simplified geometrical acoustics; whether higher physical fidelity actually helps was unclear.
*Method:* Train SpatialNet on data augmented with different room-acoustic simulators — geometrical, high-fidelity wave-based, and a wave/geometrical hybrid — then evaluate all resulting models on real measured recordings.
*Result:* Training on the high-fidelity dataset cuts median WER by up to 38% relative versus lower-fidelity augmentation, showing simulation accuracy translates directly into downstream enhancement gains.
**Why it ranked:** Squarely in core speech enhancement, from Treble Technologies (a room-acoustics simulation leader), with a clean, actionable, Interspeech-accepted result that quantifies exactly how much simulation fidelity is worth.

### 3. Self-Supervised Test-Time Tuning for Packet Loss Concealment — `2607.01823` · **Relevance 6/10 · Quality 7/10**
Yehoshua Dissen, Joseph Keshet — Technion – Israel Institute of Technology · eess.AS, cs.CL · 2026-07-02 · *Under submission to IEEE TASLP* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.01823)
*Problem:* Packet-loss concealment models stay frozen at deployment, ignoring the signal-specific cues in the packets that did arrive on each call.
*Method:* TTT-PLC adapts an existing PLC model at test time using only received packets — synthetically masking arrived audio, training to conceal it with the native objective, then reconstructing the true losses; runs in non-causal and streaming-causal modes with no clean reference.
*Result:* On FRN (speech) and PARCnet (music) backbones, per-signal test-time adaptation consistently improves concealment over the fixed pretrained models.
**Why it ranked:** A novel, cleanly-scoped test-time-adaptation idea for a real speech-restoration problem from Keshet's Technion group, validated across both speech and music backbones and streaming vs. non-causal settings.

### 4. Beyond Binary Instrument QA: Probing Instrument Grounding in Music Audio-Language Models — `2606.31338` · **Relevance 6/10 · Quality 7/10**
Yujun Lee, Joonhyeok Shin, Hyoeun Kim, Kyuhong Shim — Sungkyunkwan University · cs.SD, cs.AI, eess.AS · 2026-06-30 · *Workshop on Machine Learning for Audio, ICML 2026* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.31338)
*Problem:* Music audio-language models score high on instrument QA, but it is unclear whether that reflects real audio grounding or benchmark shortcuts.
*Method:* An OpenMIC-derived diagnostic benchmark extends binary instrument-presence QA with genre-prior-reduced examples, confusable-instrument discrimination, longer audio context, and temporal localization to probe grounding along multiple axes.
*Result:* High binary accuracy fails to predict behavior — models show option-position bias, confusable-instrument errors, and temporal response bias — arguing for multi-axis diagnostics over a single aggregate score.
**Why it ranked:** A sharp, well-designed critique of the audio-LLM hype — showing strong instrument-QA scores can hide shortcut behavior — directly relevant to the audio-understanding/audio-LLM interest, if only a workshop-scale study.

### 5. Enhancing Acoustic-to-Articulatory Inversion with Multi-Target Pretraining for Low-Resource Settings — `2607.01594` · **Relevance 6/10 · Quality 6/10**
Jesuraj Bandekar, Prasanta Kumar Ghosh — Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore (SPIRE Lab) · eess.AS · 2026-07-02 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.01594)
*Problem:* Acoustic-to-articulatory inversion benefits from SSL features, especially in low-resource settings, but SSL extractors add inference latency and compute.
*Method:* A pretraining scheme using three targets — phoneme labels, articulatory-feature labels, and critical-articulator labels — so the model needs no SSL extractor at inference time.
*Result:* Consistently improves inversion accuracy over baseline and SSL-based models, with the largest gains in low-resource conditions, while cutting inference cost without sacrificing accuracy.
**Why it ranked:** Efficient low-resource speech modeling (a core signal) with a practical contribution — dropping the SSL extractor at inference — from Ghosh's IISc SPIRE lab, though the gains are incremental rather than paradigm-shifting.
