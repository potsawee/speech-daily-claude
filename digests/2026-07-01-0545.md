### 1. ★ PRIME-Speech: Preserving Speech-to-Text LLM Capabilities in Speech-to-Speech Generation — 2606.30944 — **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 8/10**
**Authors:** Yuxuan Hu, Heng Lu, Ruchao Fan, Yao Qian, Xiaofei Wang, Jian Xue, Heming Wang, Shuohang Wang, Jinyu Li — *Microsoft* · eess.AS, cs.CL · 2026-06-29 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.30944)
*Problem:* Adding speech output to a strong speech-to-text LLM either degrades its text reasoning (if you fine-tune the backbone) or reintroduces a serial TTS bottleneck.
*Method:* A frozen-backbone framework trains only speech-generation modules; a causal audio post-decoder syncs to the backbone's intermediate hidden states, generating codec tokens from the evolving reasoning trajectory, with multi-token prediction for low first-audio latency.
*Result:* Preserves the backbone's S2T behavior across translation, spoken QA, understanding, and multi-turn dialogue while producing accurate low-WER speech.
**Why it ranked:** Directly on the user's spoken-LLM core interest, from a followed Microsoft author, with a clean frozen-backbone idea and broad evaluation — an easy group-chat share.

### 2. FlexiSLM: A Dynamic and Controllable Frame Rate Spoken Language Model — 2606.31247 — **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 8/10**
**Authors:** Jiaqi Li, Chaoren Wang, Xiaohai Tian, Mingjie Chen, Xinyu Liang, Xu Li, Yufan Lin, Junwen Qiu, et al. — *primary affiliation not stated in abstract* · cs.SD · 2026-06-30 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.31247)
*Problem:* Existing spoken LMs fix the speech frame rate (e.g. 25/12.5 Hz), ignoring speech's time-varying information density and offering no quality/speed trade-off at inference.
*Method:* The first SLM built on dynamic frame-rate speech coding for both input and output, giving very low average frame rates plus runtime frame-rate controllability.
*Result:* Beats 7B fixed-rate models (Qwen2.5-Omni, Kimi-Audio) at high-quality points; steerable down to 4.0 Hz, and at 6.25 Hz roughly halves inference time vs 12.5 Hz with strong S2S quality.
**Why it ranked:** Hits the SLM + audio-tokenizer sweet spot with a genuinely new controllability axis and credible head-to-head wins over strong 7B baselines (demo page provided).

### 3. UniSAE: Unified Speech Attribute Editing on Speaker, Emotion and Low-Level Content via Discrete Phonetic Posteriorgram Modelling — 2606.31128 — **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
**Authors:** Chuanbo Zhu, Wuyou Zhou, Rongxiu Zhong, Shilei Zhang, Kun Qian, Yike Guo, Wei Xue — *HKUST* · cs.SD · 2026-06-30 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.31128)
*Problem:* Speech editing methods treat content, speaker, and emotion as separate tasks and mostly operate only at word-level, limiting granularity and flexibility.
*Method:* A single framework using a Discrete Phonetic PosteriorGram (DPPG) that factorizes content into phoneme-identity/pronunciation/duration tokens, an autoregressive transformer for word-level edits, and a diffusion decoder conditioned on disentangled speaker/emotion.
*Result:* Composable speaker, emotion, and sub-phoneme-to-word content editing — including joint modification of all three — within one architecture.
**Why it ranked:** Spans TTS/VC/emotion-editing interests with a neat unifying representation from the HKUST (Wei Xue/Yike Guo) group; edging on Quality only because the abstract reports capability more than hard benchmark numbers.

### 4. BEST-RQ-2: Contextualize-Then-Predict, a Two-Step Approach for Self-Supervised Audio Representations — 2606.30700 — **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 8/10**
**Authors:** Ludovic K. Tuncay, Etienne Labbé, Thomas Pellegrini — *IRIT-SAMoVA (Université de Toulouse)* · cs.SD · 2026-06-29 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.30700)
*Problem:* BEST-RQ's single-stage masked prediction couples contextualization and target prediction, limiting transfer across speech, music, and environmental sound.
*Method:* Keeps frozen random-projection discrete targets but splits pretraining into contextualize-then-predict — a ViT context encoder over unmasked regions plus a lightweight predictor (discarded after pretraining) for masked targets.
*Result:* On X-ARES / X-ARES-LLM it consistently beats one-stage baselines in overall transfer at unchanged inference compute, improving music/environmental sound; code and checkpoints released.
**Why it ranked:** A principled, reproducible advance on a widely-used SSL recipe with released models — high Quality; Relevance moderate since it targets general audio SSL rather than the user's speech-generation core.

### 5. SwiftAudio: Data-Efficient Caption-Only Distillation for One-Step Text-to-Audio Diffusion — 2606.31259 — **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7/10**
**Authors:** Binh Mai, Tran Quoc Bao Le, Hung Dinh, Cong Tran — *primary affiliation not stated in abstract* · cs.SD · 2026-06-30 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.31259)
*Problem:* Diffusion TTA is slow (iterative denoising), and existing one-step distillations still need paired text–audio data.
*Method:* Audio-free distillation from a pretrained diffusion teacher using only ~45K captions, adapting Variational Score Distillation to audio with a temporal-smoothness regularizer for coherent latents.
*Result:* SOTA among strict one-step methods on AudioCaps and Clotho, substantially narrowing the gap to multi-step diffusion.
**Why it ranked:** Fits the diffusion/flow-audio and efficiency interests with a clever caption-only distillation trick and honest one-step SOTA framing; Relevance/Quality moderate as it targets general text-to-audio rather than speech and comes from a less-established group.
