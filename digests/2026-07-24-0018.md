### 1. ★ SimulS2ST-Omni: Data-Efficient Streaming Speech-to-Speech Translation via Explicit Trajectory Supervision — `2607.19810` · **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Rongshen He, Xinyu Liang, Dekun Chen, Jiaqi Li, Mingjie Chen, **Zhizheng Wu** (The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen) · cs.SD · 2026-07-22*
[Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.19810)

*Problem:* Long-form streaming speech-to-speech translation needs unbounded, low-latency output but existing methods are sentence-bounded or demand massive paired S2ST data.
*Method:* A speech LM trained on only ~2k hours of paired data via joint text-code trajectory supervision (a unified emission path) plus a two-stream Thinker–Talker factorization that decouples reasoning from acoustic prediction.
*Result:* Matches closed-source SOTA (LiveInterpret 2.0) on RealSI and ACL60/60 ASR-BLEU, staying robust even with the paired budget cut 90%.
**Why it ranked:** Core streaming-S2ST/speech-LM topic from followed author Zhizheng Wu, with an ambitious data-efficiency result that reaches closed-source quality — a thorough, credible 29-page study.

### 2. ★ Efficient Chain-of-Modality Reasoning via Progressive Compression for Spoken Language Models — `2607.19932` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Pengchao Feng, Chao-Hong Tan, Qian Chen, Wen Wang, Xiangang Li, **Xie Chen** (Shanghai Jiao Tong University, X-LANCE; Alibaba Token Foundry) · cs.CL/cs.SD · 2026-07-22*
[Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.19932)

*Problem:* Spoken language models reason poorly over verbalized math versus symbolic text, yet transferring text reasoning is costly and architecturally awkward.
*Method:* ECoM Reasoning compresses the textual reasoning trace so it doubles as speech guidance, trained via Progressive Compression — a curriculum easing from full-form to compressed reasoning.
*Result:* +21% accuracy over CoM without reasoning and +3% over full-trace CoM while using only 40% of the text tokens, improving accuracy and inference efficiency together.
**Why it ranked:** Directly on the spoken-LM core interest from followed author Xie Chen (SJTU × Alibaba), with a genuinely novel compressed-reasoning idea and clean, quantified accuracy-vs-cost gains.

### 3. PINT: Content is What Remains — Invariant Speech Tokenization from Parallel Utterances — `2607.19033` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Laurin Wagner, Bernhard Thallinger, Miroslav Stankovic, Mario Zusag (nyra labs) · cs.CL · 2026-07-21*
[Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.19033)

*Problem:* Discrete speech tokenizers (e.g. HuBERT targets) leak speaker, prosody, and channel info into tokens, inflating entropy and hurting downstream codecs.
*Method:* PINT fine-tunes an SSL encoder with alignment losses across parallel utterances of the same words, distilling the only shared factor — linguistic content — into invariant tokens that keep frame-level timing.
*Result:* 98.7% relative drop in speaker-probe accuracy (93.1%→1.2%), 42% lower ABX error, and 27–30% lower LM perplexity versus baselines.
**Why it ranked:** A standout non-followed paper (Interspeech 2026) landing squarely on the codec/tokenizer interest — a crisp "the right invariance" insight backed by large, clean disentanglement gains.

### 4. ★ StellarTTS: Sparse Temporal Embedding for Low-Latency and Robust Speech Synthesis — `2607.19859` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Kaicheng Luo, Xuefei Gong, Yutao Sun, Jinling He, Yujie Hou, Xiaoyang Xing, Huiyan Li, Bing Han, **Yanmin Qian** (Shanghai Jiao Tong University, X-LANCE) · cs.SD · 2026-07-22*
[Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.19859)

*Problem:* TTS must trade off robustness, latency, and prosody — autoregressive models are slow/error-prone while non-autoregressive ones flatten prosody with rigid alignments.
*Method:* A mobile-optimized NAR framework using a sparse temporal embedding for granular duration/pronunciation/prosody control, plus a semantic-aware codec enabling single-stage decoding on an 83M-param masked generative transformer.
*Result:* Real-time factor 0.08 with lower latency and stronger robustness than SOTA TTS, holding competitive audio quality, prosody, and speaker similarity.
**Why it ranked:** Core on-device TTS from followed author Yanmin Qian (SJTU), ASRU 2025-accepted with concrete real-time and robustness wins — solid engineering, if more optimization than paradigm shift.

### 5. ★ Pushing the Frontier of Full-Song Generation: Hierarchical Autoregressive Planning Meets Flow-Matching Rendering — `2607.20253` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Junyu Dai, Xinyue Fan, Weiqin Li, Xiangang Li, Yunjia Li, Bin Ma, Yukun Ma, Chongjia Ni, Yufei Shi, Haoxu Wang, Menglin Wu, Jianwei Yu, Huaicheng Zhang, Han Zhao, Shengkui Zhao, Haina Zhu (Alibaba Token Foundry / Tongyi) · cs.SD/cs.AI/eess.AS · 2026-07-22*
[Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.20253)

*Problem:* Generating high-quality full-length songs from lyrics, descriptions, and attributes — across lyrics-to-song, instrumental, and cover tasks — remains hard to unify.
*Method:* A four-part system: an 8-codebook RVQ semantic tokenizer, a hierarchical autoregressive hybrid-LM, a FullDiT flow-matching renderer in continuous VAE latent space, and a melody module, refined with DPO/GRPO reward post-training.
*Result:* Competitive quality on a multilingual benchmark and the Artificial Analysis "Music with Vocals" leaderboard across all three generation tasks.
**Why it ranked:** Music is a secondary interest, but this is a standout-quality, ambitious flagship system from followed lab Alibaba — the kind of comprehensive report worth sharing even outside your core focus.
