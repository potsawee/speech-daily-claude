### 1. ★ MuScriptor: An Open Model for Multi-Instrument Music Transcription — `2607.08168` · **Relevance 6/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Simon Rouard, Michael Krause, Axel Roebel, Carl-Johann Simon-Gabriel, **Alexandre Défossez** (Kyutai; IRCAM — Sorbonne Université) · cs.SD/cs.LG · 2026-07-09 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.08168)*

*Problem:* Automatic music transcription is largely stuck on single-instrument or clean recordings and fails on real multi-instrument mixes; models trained only on synthetic audio generalize poorly, giving near-unusable output on real music.
*Method:* Combine synthetic-data pretraining with fine-tuning on real music audio and reinforcement-learning post-training, plus conditioning on instrument presence to customize the transcription.
*Result:* Releases MuScriptor, an open-weight multi-instrument transcription model that works on real-world recordings across a diverse range of genres.
**Why it ranked:** Followed author Défossez and a follow-lab (Kyutai, with IRCAM) shipping an *open-weight* model on a genuinely new synthetic-pretrain → real-finetune → RL recipe; music transcription is a secondary interest, but the release and recipe make it a clear share.

### 2. ★ MulTTiPop: A Multitrack Transcription Dataset for Pop Music — `2607.08756` · **Relevance 5/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Nathan Pruyne, Benjamin Stoler, William Chen, Chien-yu Huang, **Shinji Watanabe**, Chris Donahue (Carnegie Mellon University — G-CLef & WAVLab; National Taiwan University) · cs.SD/cs.LG · 2026-07-09 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.08756)*

*Problem:* Automatic music transcription lacks a realistic multitrack pop benchmark; existing evaluation is thin and pop-biased, obscuring how far models are from usable real-world transcription.
*Method:* Metadata-match segments from Lakh MIDI and TheoryTab to audio, manually anchor one beat, then beat-track the audio and warp the MIDI to align 572 pop segments (3.5h, spanning the 1930s–2000s) with their multitrack MIDI.
*Result:* Even SOTA transcribers reach only 38% Onset F1, exposing large headroom.
**Why it ranked:** Followed author Shinji Watanabe (CMU G-CLef/WAVLab); a carefully constructed eval set with a memorable SOTA-gap number, though music transcription sits in the user's secondary interests.

### 3. When Synthetic Speech Is All You Have: Better Call GRPO — `2607.08409` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Shashi Kumar, Yanis Labrak, Hasindri Watawana, Sergio Burdisso, Esaú Villatoro-Tello, Kadri Hacıoğlu, Petr Motlicek, Andreas Stolcke (Idiap Research Institute; Uniphore) · cs.CL/cs.AI · 2026-07-09 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.08409)*

*Problem:* LLM-based ASR for regulated domains like banking is bottlenecked by privacy — real speech is costly and legally constrained — so synthetic TTS is used, but it stays acoustically mismatched and prior work only did supervised fine-tuning (SFT).
*Method:* Apply GRPO, a critic-free RL method rewarding low-WER hypotheses, to adapt on synthetic speech, and compare head-to-head against SFT.
*Result:* GRPO cuts WER 40% relative to SFT (36.71%→22.09%), and SFT-then-GRPO reaches 45%; gains trace to better stopping calibration and audio-anchored attention, leaving early-layer representations intact.
**Why it ranked:** Core ASR relevance with a large, clean result and a mechanistic "why," submitted to SLT 2026 from a credible Idiap/Uniphore team (Andreas Stolcke).

### 4. Structural Bottlenecks on Frequency Representation in End-to-End Audio Models — `2607.08545` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Nicole Cosme-Clifford (Yale University) · cs.SD/cs.LG · 2026-07-09 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.08545)*

*Problem:* The strided-convolution encoders behind neural audio codecs and generators may not actually preserve access to interpretable time-frequency primitives (pitch, timbre), even when reconstruction looks good.
*Method:* Theory plus controlled experiments identify two architecture-predictable bottlenecks — aliasing collapse of primitives and limited filter frequency resolution — then Gabor Latent Refactorization (GLRF), a retraining-free re-expression of encoder latents in a frequency-localized basis.
*Result:* Measures 31–35% primitive collapse and 10–35× over-wide filters; GLRF shrinks bandwidth to 1.5–3× the resolution bound while preserving fidelity and improving pitch control.
**Why it ranked:** Standout single-author (Yale) theory-plus-fix landing squarely on the user's audio-codec/tokenization interest, handing over a practical, retraining-free steerability tool.

### 5. Best-of-N TTS Evaluation is Confounded by ASR Family Alignment — `2607.08256` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Taehyung Yu, Seongjae Kang (affiliations not stated on the paper) · cs.CL/cs.SD · 2026-07-09 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.08256)*

*Problem:* Best-of-N zero-shot TTS picks among candidates with an ASR verifier, but a verifier's apparent quality may depend on which ASR *family* judges it — an unexamined evaluation confound.
*Method:* On LibriSpeech-PC with F5-TTS, cross Whisper / wav2vec 2.0 / HuBERT as verifiers and evaluators, and propose two cross-family rank ensembles (rank-averaging and conjunctive max-rank).
*Result:* Verifier rankings *reverse* across evaluator families despite CKA 0.978; cross-family ensembles reach 1.61% WER at N=10 (−12% vs F5-TTS) with no SIM-o/UTMOS degradation.
**Why it ranked:** A directly relevant TTS-evaluation gotcha with a surprising, actionable finding (same-family verifier–evaluator coupling inflates BoN gains) plus a concrete fix; ICML 2026 workshop, though authorship/affiliation is undisclosed.
