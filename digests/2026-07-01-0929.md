### 1. ★ TF-MoE: Time-Frequency Mixture-of-Experts for Efficient Speech Separation — arXiv:2606.29575 — **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 8/10**
**Authors:** Qinzhe Hu, Chenda Li, Wangyou Zhang, Yanmin Qian (Shanghai Jiao Tong University); Shujie Liu, Yan Lu (Microsoft Research Asia) · cs.SD, cs.AI · 2026-06-28 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.29575)
*Problem:* Compact speech-separation front-ends are cheap in parameters but still too compute-heavy for edge devices.
*Method:* A sparse MoE over a mel-band Conformer, alternating time-wise and frequency-wise expert routing that selects experts per frame or mel band, raising capacity at near-flat inference cost.
*Result:* +3.8 dB SDR over BSRNN on Libri2Mix at a comparable 4.1 GMACs/s, targeting on-device deployment.
**Why it ranked:** Core source-separation topic from a followed lab (Microsoft) with a concrete SOTA-under-compute-budget result and a clean, deployable idea.

### 2. DTM-Codec: Dynamic Token Masking for VFR Speech Coding with Efficient Boundary Selection — arXiv:2606.29480 — **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
**Authors:** Hoyeol Sohn, Juhan Nam (KAIST) · eess.AS, cs.SD · 2026-06-28 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.29480)
*Problem:* Variable-frame-rate neural codecs claim savings, but reported gains often vanish once the side-information bits for retained frames are counted.
*Method:* Dynamic Token Masking keeps selected encoder tokens, fills the rest with a learned <MASK> embedding, and transmits a binary keep-mask for position-aware decoding, plus a linear-time Path Length Equalization boundary selector.
*Result:* Clear reconstruction-quality and intelligibility gains over fixed-frame-rate baselines under a strict matched-total-bitrate protocol.
**Why it ranked:** Directly on the neural-codec/tokenizer interest, and its honest matched-bitrate accounting fixes a real credibility gap in prior VFR work.

### 3. AVTok: 1D Unified Tokenization for Holistic Audio-Video Generation — arXiv:2606.30811 — **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 8/10**
**Authors:** Kien T. Pham, I Chieh Chen, Qifeng Chen, Long Chen (HKUST) · cs.CV, cs.MM, cs.SD, eess.AS · 2026-06-29 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.30811)
*Problem:* Audio-video generators use separate per-modality tokenizers, ignoring the representation gap and burning compute.
*Method:* A dual-stream transformer with a shared encoder-decoder and modal-specific learnable queries encodes an audio-video pair into a compact 1D latent over a unified codebook, trained with a hierarchical reconstruction curriculum.
*Result:* Strong audio-video reconstruction and gains when plugged into audio-to-video, video-to-audio, and joint class-conditional generation pipelines.
**Why it ranked:** Ambitious unified-tokenizer direction squarely in the audio-tokenizer interest; novel single-codebook A/V design with broad downstream validation.

### 4. VIB-AVSR: Variational Information Bottleneck for Noise-Robust LLM-Based Audio-Visual Speech Recognition — arXiv:2606.29632 — **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7/10**
**Authors:** Piyush Arora, Navlika Singh, Umberto Cappellazzo, Stavros Petridis, Maja Pantic (Imperial College London) · eess.AS, cs.CV, cs.SD · 2026-06-28 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.29632)
*Problem:* LLM-based AVSR models excel in clean audio but have no explicit mechanism for stable representations under noise, so they degrade badly when audio is corrupted.
*Method:* Insert Variational Information Bottleneck layers at targeted positions inside the LLM backbone to regularize representations, with no architectural change or extra training data.
*Result:* Reduced degradation across multiple SNR levels and noise types relative to standard LLM-AVSR.
**Why it ranked:** Timely LLM-ASR robustness angle from a strong AVSR group (Petridis/Pantic); method is lightweight though the abstract omits hard numbers.

### 5. ALM2Vec: Learning Audio Embeddings for Universal Audio Retrieval with Large Audio-Language Models — arXiv:2606.30682 — **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7/10**
**Authors:** Fengjie Lu, Chenang Jiang, Jiarui Hai, Helin Wang, Aaron Yee (Johns Hopkins University) · cs.SD, cs.AI, eess.AS · 2026-06-27 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.30682)
*Problem:* Contrastive dual-encoder audio embeddings are tuned for caption matching, so they can't support diverse or instruction-conditioned retrieval.
*Method:* Derive a universal embedding space from pretrained large audio-language models, transferring their understanding, instruction-following, and reasoning to enable instruction-aware retrieval (e.g., audio QA, aspect-conditioned).
*Result:* Competitive on standard audio/speech retrieval benchmarks while adding compositional and controllable retrieval behaviors.
**Why it ranked:** Sits at the audio-LLM × retrieval intersection the profile favors; a genuinely new capability, though results are "competitive" rather than clearly SOTA.
