### 1. ★ Unified Audio Intelligence Without Regressing on Text Intelligence (Audex) — `2607.05196` · **Relevance 9/10 · Quality 9/10**
Zhifeng Kong, Sang-gil Lee, Jaehyeon Kim, Boxin Wang, Zihan Liu, Sungwon Kim, Yang Chen, Arushi Goel, Wenliang Dai, Sreyan Ghosh, Andrew Tao, Mohammad Shoeybi, Bryan Catanzaro, Wei Ping — NVIDIA (Nemotron-Labs) · cs.CL, cs.AI, cs.LG, cs.SD, eess.AS · 2026-07-06 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.05196)
*Problem:* Audio LLMs that bolt on speech/audio understanding and generation typically regress on the base model's text intelligence and fuse the two modalities awkwardly.
*Method:* Audex is a 30B-MoE unified audio-text LLM on a Nemotron text backbone — a single Transformer decoder projects encoded audio into the text embedding space and treats text and quantized audio-output tokens uniformly, trained on 157.4B audio + 320.5B text tokens with multi-stage SFT, text-only RL, and on-policy distillation.
*Result:* Delivers strong unified audio understanding, reasoning, and generation while explicitly preserving text-only performance.
**Why it ranked:** ★ followed (Sreyan Ghosh) and an NVIDIA flagship on exactly the audio-LLM / spoken-language-model frontier the user tracks — ambitious scale, a clean unified single-decoder design, and a rare, explicit no-text-regression claim; also the day's top Hugging Face audio paper.

### 2. DELTA-TTS: Adapting Autoregressive Model into Diffusion Language Model for Text-to-Speech — `2607.04140` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 8/10**
Junwon Moon, Seungbeom Kim, Yejin Lee, Hoseong Ahn, Sewoong Park, Heeseung Kim, Kyuhong Shim — Sungkyunkwan University (SKKU) · eess.AS, cs.CL · 2026-07-05 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.04140)
*Problem:* Autoregressive TTS decodes speech tokens strictly left-to-right, making inference slow and propagating local errors and hallucinations — even though the full input text is known before synthesis.
*Method:* A lightweight LoRA adapter converts a pretrained AR TTS model into a discrete diffusion LM for confidence-ordered token decoding, adding a convolution module for local acoustic context, a 1/t-weighted training objective, and a time-shifted inference schedule that defers low-confidence positions.
*Result:* Trained on only 585h of LibriTTS, it hits 1.75% WER on Seed-TTS test-en, beating its own AR backbone while decoding tokens non-sequentially.
**Why it ranked:** Standout-quality core-TTS work — a genuinely novel AR→diffusion adaptation that is parameter-efficient, data-light, and posts a strong intelligibility number; the kind of clean method result a TTS group circulates.

### 3. DuplexChat: Constructing Speaker-Separated Full-Duplex Dialogue Speech at Scale — `2607.04941` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
Wataru Nakata, Yuki Saito, Hiroshi Saruwatari — The University of Tokyo · cs.CL, cs.SD, eess.AS · 2026-07-06 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.04941)
*Problem:* Full-duplex spoken dialogue models need each speaker on a separate stream, but large public speech corpora are mostly monaural and thus unsuited for training such models.
*Method:* DuplexChat-Pipe filters language-specific podcast feeds, retrieves and cleans episode audio, extracts diarization-guided two-speaker clips, and applies speech separation plus restoration to produce one clean channel per speaker.
*Result:* Yields an open corpus of 282,634h English and 132,723h Japanese speaker-separated dialogue that retains the turn-taking dynamics of human conversation.
**Why it ranked:** A massive, openly released resource squarely on the hot full-duplex spoken-dialogue-LM frontier from Saruwatari's U-Tokyo lab — the scarce ingredient (speaker-separated conversational speech at scale) that many SDLM efforts are bottlenecked on.

### 4. ★ Listen, Think, Transcribe: Continuous Latent Test-Time Scaling for ASR (LatentASR) — `2607.05051` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 8/10**
Ho Lam Chung, Yiming Chen, Dau-Cheng Lyu, Hsiao-Tsung Hung, Hung-yi Lee — National Taiwan University · cs.SD · 2026-07-06 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.05051)
*Problem:* End-to-end ASR transcribes in a single pass, giving the decoder no way to revisit hard inputs, and standard adaptation on tiny data regresses WER.
*Method:* LatentASR adds continuous-latent test-time scaling atop a fully frozen Qwen3-ASR-0.6B via two small (~4M-param) modules — a Latent Adapter that iteratively refines a few latent prefix positions with bounded updates, and a Value Head that predicts benefit and halts the loop early; trained on just 500 diverse utterances.
*Result:* It is the only tested method to lower WER on clean sets (FLEURS −2.54%, VoxPopuli −0.47% rel.) under the minimal-data regime, where full FT, LoRA, and prompt tuning all increase WER.
**Why it ranked:** ★ followed (Hung-yi Lee) and the day's most novel ASR idea — bringing test-time "thinking" compute to speech recognition, parameter-efficiently, with an honest minimal-data comparison in which every standard baseline regresses.

### 5. SPEARBench: A Benchmark for Naturalness Evaluation in Streaming Speech-to-Speech Language Models — `2607.05365` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7/10**
Thomas Thebaud, Yuzhe Wang, Hao Zhang, Sathvik Manikantan Napa Ugandhar, Ashish Hallur, Georgi Tinchev, Venkatesh Ravichandran, Laureano Moro-Velazquez — Johns Hopkins University (CLSP) with industry co-authors · cs.CL, cs.AI, eess.AS · 2026-07-06 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.05365)
*Problem:* Standard speech and text benchmarks don't capture whether streaming speech-to-speech LMs behave naturally in conversation, where timing, turn-taking, prosody, interpersonal stance, and language/dialect consistency jointly shape perceived quality.
*Method:* SPEARBench builds controlled dialogue prompts from the Seamless Interaction corpus, runs inference across several S2S models, and scores answers on a multidimensional protocol — latency, interruptions, speech quality, ASR robustness, language/dialect consistency, emotional naturalness, interpersonal stance — against human-answer references.
*Result:* Finds that current models can reach high signal-level quality yet still fall short on the conversational-naturalness dimensions.
**Why it ranked:** Directly serves the speech-LM interest — evaluation for the very S2S frontier that Audex and DuplexChat target — a well-motivated multi-axis naturalness benchmark from JHU CLSP that names concrete failure modes rather than reporting a single aggregate score.
