### 1. OLIVE: View-Augmented Latent Prediction with Waveform Reconstruction for Speech SSL — `2606.30356` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Karl El Hajal, Mathew Magimai.-Doss (Idiap Research Institute & EPFL, Switzerland) · cs.CL / cs.LG / cs.SD / eess.AS · 2026-06-29* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.30356)

*Problem:* Speech SSL usually optimizes either analysis (recognition/semantic) objectives or synthesis, so no single representation serves both understanding and generation well.
*Method:* OLIVE jointly trains view-augmented masked latent prediction with waveform reconstruction under one objective — reconstruction keeps signal detail in early layers, while latent prediction pushes later layers toward invariance.
*Result:* One representation improves generation and speaker tasks plus waveform reconstruction, while staying competitive on recognition and semantic benchmarks.
**Why it ranked:** Core self-supervised-speech territory with a genuinely unifying analysis+synthesis objective from Idiap (Magimai-Doss), a credible group — a clean, shareable idea rather than another incremental pretext task.

### 2. Adaptive Perturbation Selection for Contrastive Audio Decoding — `2607.00247` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Aaron Isidore Grace, Weiran Wang (University of Iowa), Zhouyuan Huo (Google) · cs.SD / cs.AI · 2026-06-30* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.00247)

*Problem:* Large audio-language models hallucinate by overriding acoustic evidence with language priors, and existing contrastive-decoding fixes rely on blunt masking or noise.
*Method:* They evaluate a library of targeted audio transforms across temporal/spectral/frequency/amplitude domains, add a binary yes/no prompt constraint, and train a light selector on hidden states to route the best negative branch per example.
*Result:* Reversing the audio lifts temporal-order accuracy 74.7%→81.4%, and the learned selector adds +4.3% on the existence task — all training-free at the base model.
**Why it ranked:** Squarely on audio-LLMs (a top interest) with a practical, training-free hallucination fix and honest task-dependent analysis; a solid contribution with released details.

### 3. Is Natural Always Appropriate? Investigating Naturalness and Appropriateness Across Domains for TTS Evaluation — `2606.31729` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7/10**
*Dominika Woszczyk (Iconic, UK), Andreas Triantafyllopoulos, Björn Schuller (TU Munich & Imperial College London), Jura Miniota, Éva Székely (KTH Royal Institute of Technology) · eess.AS / cs.LG · 2026-06-30* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.31729)

*Problem:* TTS "naturalness" is treated as near-solved, but whether synthetic speech is *appropriate* for its downstream context is largely unmeasured.
*Method:* Rate the appropriateness and human-likeness of five SOTA TTS systems across five domains — AI assistant, reader, actor, animated character, and spontaneous speaker.
*Result:* Appropriateness varies by domain independently of naturalness; expressive domains stay hard, optimizing one degrades others, and naturalness scores penalize stylized speech while rewarding spontaneity.
**Why it ranked:** Directly targets the user's #1 area (TTS) with a timely, well-designed perceptual study that punctures the "naturalness is solved" narrative — a strong discussion-starter for a synthesis group.

### 4. VeRe-Flow: Guiding Flow Matching toward Clean Speech for Noise-Robust Bandwidth Expansion — `2606.29450` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 6/10**
*Sujin Koo (MAGO), Sangyoon Kim, Ji Sub Um, Hoirin Kim (KAIST), South Korea · eess.AS · 2026-06-28* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.29450)

*Problem:* Flow matching excels at speech generation but struggles at noise-robust bandwidth expansion because velocity estimation is ambiguous under noise.
*Method:* VeRe-Flow adds multi-level clean supervision — velocity contrastive regularization pulls the predicted velocity toward the clean trajectory and away from noisy ones, plus representation alignment to clean SSL features.
*Result:* Achieves the lowest LSD and highest DNSMOS OVRL among all baselines, and the best MOS among generative baselines.
**Why it ranked:** Hits the user's flow-matching-for-speech and enhancement interests with a clean, SOTA generative BWE result — though the contribution is a focused refinement rather than a new paradigm, which caps quality.

### 5. Semi-Supervised Sound Event Detection with Conditional Mixup and Embedding-Level Contrastive Loss — `2606.29901` · **Relevance 6/10 · Quality 8/10**
*Nian Shao, Xian Li, Xiaofei Li (Westlake University & Zhejiang University, China) · eess.AS / cs.AI · 2026-06-29* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.29901)

*Problem:* SED is bottlenecked by scarce labels; fine-tuning audio foundation models must exploit abundant unlabeled data, but mixup plays conflicting roles in pseudo-labeling versus contrastive learning.
*Method:* Extend the ATST-SED semi-supervised framework with an embedding-level self-supervised contrastive loss and a "conditional mixup" that unifies composition mixup (for pseudo-labels) and perturbation mixup (for contrast).
*Result:* Reaches 0.645 PSDS1 and 0.822 PSDS2 on DESED — a new state of the art.
**Why it ranked:** Sound event detection is only a secondary interest, but this is a crisp, clearly-SOTA fix to a real mixup mismatch from Xiaofei Li's respected Westlake audio group — standout quality earns the slot under the "great-quality even if moderately relevant" rule.
