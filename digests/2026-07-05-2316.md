### 1. Decomposer: Learning to Decompile Symbolic Music to Programs — `2607.01849` · **Relevance 6/10 · Quality 8/10**
Yewon Kim, Apurva Gandhi, David Chung, Graham Neubig, Chris Donahue — Carnegie Mellon University · cs.LG, cs.AI, cs.SD · 2026-07-02 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.01849)
*Problem:* Recovering the high-level, editable instructions behind a musical performance is a hard inverse problem; no model reliably turns symbolic MIDI back into readable, re-runnable code.
*Method:* Two-stage "decompilation" of MIDI into Strudel programs — supervised fine-tuning on a synthetic paired corpus (Strudel-Synth), then RL on unpaired MIDI rewarding both reconstruction faithfulness and code readability.
*Result:* Beats closed-source LLMs on MIDI reconstruction faithfulness while producing more readable, diverse programs than the heuristic converter, across synthetic and real-world MIDI.
**Why it ranked:** A genuinely novel task with a clean two-stage recipe and honest comparisons from a strong group (Neubig/Donahue, CMU) lifts quality above the day's field — enough to carry it in despite symbolic music being only a secondary-interest fit.

### 2. RT-Tango: Real-Time Distributed Binaural Speech Enhancement for Low-Power Hearing Aid Devices — `2607.01834` · **Relevance 8/10 · Quality 7/10**
Z. Benslimane, P. Chouteau, M. Poreba, F. Auzanneau, M. Szczepanski, F. Chersi, Romain Serizel — CEA-List & Université de Lorraine (Loria) · cs.SD · 2026-07-02 · *Accepted at Interspeech 2026* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.01834)
*Problem:* Binaural speech enhancement on hearing aids is throttled by latency, compute, and the wireless link between ear devices, and efficient methods so far mostly handle single-channel input.
*Method:* A two-stage distributed pipeline — ERB feature compression, lightweight grouped recurrent mask estimation, temporal sparsification, plus an asymmetric STFT that decouples spectral resolution from algorithmic delay with causal, online spatial statistics.
*Result:* Competitive enhancement at ultra-low 8 ms latency while cutting MAC operations substantially, fitting resource-constrained hearing-aid hardware.
**Why it ranked:** Squarely in the core speech-enhancement interest and hitting the streaming/on-device priority signal; a well-scoped, credibly evaluated Interspeech system, if incremental rather than paradigm-shifting.

### 3. Proteus: Automated Adversarial Robustness Testing for Audio Deepfake Detectors — `2606.29544` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 7/10**
Nicolas M. Müller, Aditya Tirumala Bukkapatnam, Zohaib Ahmed — Resemble AI · cs.SD, cs.AI · 2026-06-28 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.29544)
*Problem:* Audio deepfake detectors can be fooled by ordinary, quality-preserving audio processing, yet finding which transformation chains break a given detector is largely unautomated.
*Method:* Proteus searches sequences of everyday transforms (codec transcoding, noise, reverb, dynamic-range compression, VoIP) with a breadth-first sweep plus a Q-learning agent that chains deeper attacks from the BFS structure.
*Result:* In continuous production deployment, specific augmentation chains reliably flip detection verdicts while preserving intelligibility and speaker identity, and then guide targeted retraining to harden the detector.
**Why it ranked:** A practical, deployed red-teaming tool for anti-spoofing (a core speaker-security interest) from an industry lab and a respected anti-spoofing author, with honest reporting of real failure modes.

### 4. AmbiDrop: Ambisonics-Based Array-Agnostic Neural Speech Enhancement — `2607.00548` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 6/10**
Michael Tatarjitzky, Vladimir Tourbabin, Boaz Rafaely — Ben-Gurion University of the Negev & Meta Reality Labs · eess.AS · 2026-07-01 · *Submitted to IEEE TASLP* · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.00548)
*Problem:* Multichannel speech-enhancement DNNs overfit to a fixed microphone-array geometry and generalize poorly to unseen or irregular arrays.
*Method:* Feed the network geometry-independent ideal Ambisonics and add a channel-wise dropout during training to simulate encoding errors; at inference, map arbitrary arrays into the Ambisonics domain via Ambisonics Signal Matching.
*Result:* Robust across diverse unseen simulated and real arrays, resilient to sensor failures, and effective at reduced network scale — suited to edge and wearable devices.
**Why it ranked:** A clean, general fix for the array-geometry generalization problem in core speech enhancement with credible authorship (Rafaely + Meta Reality Labs), though it is largely an analysis/extension of an existing framework rather than a new idea.

### 5. Building an ASR Solution for Training and Assessing Children's Reading — `2606.31508` · **Relevance 7/10 · Quality 6/10**
Yacouba Diarra, Nouhoum Souleymane Coulibaly, Mamadou Dembele, Aymane Dembele, Michael Leventhal — RobotsMali · cs.CL, cs.SD · 2026-06-30 · [Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.31508)
*Problem:* Children's-reading ASR for literacy assessment barely exists for African languages such as Bambara, lacking data, benchmarks, and adapted models.
*Method:* An end-to-end pipeline: 55 h of reading speech field-collected from 60 children, a new public benchmark, and a Bambara-adapted Fast-Conformer ("Soloni", TDT/CTC) compared against compact QuartzNet, with SpecAugment and repeated-reading analysis.
*Result:* Soloni cuts WER 0.42→0.22 and CER 0.15→0.08, beating QuartzNet; ten classroom trials validated the reading app, with under-10 readers the main residual error source.
**Why it ranked:** Real-world low-resource ASR (a core interest) done end-to-end — data, public benchmark, model, and classroom deployment — a genuinely useful, honest contribution, even if it adapts existing architectures rather than inventing them.
